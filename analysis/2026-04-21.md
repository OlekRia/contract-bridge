## Conclusions

### What Went Well

1. **Defence excellence (65.4%)** -- Generated 6 of 9 tops. Boards 4 (+300), 7 (3NT-2),
   11 (+300 doubled), 17 (4S-2), 21 (precision 1NT), all outstanding.
2. **Ori's declarer play (75.0%)** -- Board 12 (5SX+1), 13 (2H+1), 22 (1H+3, 2 above DD).
   Consistent excellence under pressure.
3. **Competitive bidding improved** -- Board 8 (Urs competitive 2S steal), board 13
   (Ori competitive 2H entry). No boards lost to passivity.
4. **Strong closing (R6 = 75.0%)** -- 3 tops and 1 average in the final round. Clutch
   performance when it mattered.

### What Went Wrong

1. **Urs's overbidding pattern (32.1% declaring)** -- 4 game-level overbids when partscore
   was the limit (boards 5, 10, 14, 18). Each time: long suit but insufficient HCP/controls.
   Combined cost: ~9 MP. This is the single biggest improvement opportunity.
2. **Board 6 overbid** -- Ori's 4H when 3H was the limit. -4 MP. Even strong declarers
   overbid sometimes.
3. **Slam bidding** -- Board 1 was a cold grand with 27 HCP and 8 spades. Only 1 miss
   (improved from T7's 3 misses) but still a gap when the big hands appear.
4. **Round 5 collapse (43.8%)** -- Against the eventual winners, we had 2 bottoms.
   Board 18 (5C overbid) and board 19 (opponents' 3NT made) cost us 1st place.

### Priority Actions

1. **URGENT: Address Urs's overbidding pattern.** 4 game-level overbids when partscore
   was the limit. Drill: before bidding game with a long suit but <10 HCP, count total
   tricks and compare to contract level. If DD says 9 tricks, do not bid game.
2. **Slam exploration tools.** Board 1 was a cold grand with 27 HCP and 8 spades. After
   establishing a game-forcing spade fit, need a mechanism to explore slam. Consider
   adding Kickback or RKC agreements for specific sequences.
3. **Maintain defensive excellence.** 65.4% defending is series-leading. The defence
   recovery from T7's 46.6% proves this is our core strength.
4. **Reduce variance.** 9 tops + 5 bottoms = high variance. Converting even 2 bottoms
   to averages would have won the tournament outright (55 + 4 = 59 > 56.6).
