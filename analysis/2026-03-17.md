## Key Findings

### Strengths

1. **Defence is elite.** Boards 1, 8, 14, 15 — consistently set contracts
   or held them to minimum tricks. The ♠J lead on board 14 and the ♠A
   lead on board 15 were particularly sharp.

2. **Partscore battles won.** Boards 7, 8, 16 — we found the right level
   and played accurately, outscoring the field in competitive auctions.

3. **Opponent errors exploited.** Boards 19, 20, 26 — when opponents
   underbid or misbid, we capitalised with tops even when we didn't do
   anything special.

4. **Strong closer.** Rounds 4, 5, 7 scored 56/72 (77.8%) — tournament
   was won in the second half.

### Weaknesses

1. **Board 13: Game missed with 28 HCP.** North opens 1♥ (17 HCP, 5♥),
   South has 13 HCP + 3♥ support = 28 combined. Stopping at 3♥ is a
   clear error. **Action: review forcing sequences after 1♥ opening.**
   With 13 HCP and 3-card support, responder must force to game.

2. **Board 6: 3NT missed.** South has 17 HCP + 6♦, North has 9 HCP + 7♣.
   Combined 26 HCP with two long minor suits = 3NT is cold. Stopping in
   3♦ was a failure to recognise the NT potential. **Action: when both
   hands have long minors, think NT first.**

3. **Board 10: Bottom despite correct defence.** We set 3NT but scored 0.0
   because the field made 4♥. This is a movement artefact — nothing to
   fix, just bad luck.

### Patterns Across Tournaments

| Pattern | T1 (56%) | T2 (70%) | T3 (60%) | T4 (68%) |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| Defence | Strong | Elite | Strong | Elite |
| Opening leads | Weak (bottoms) | Good | Good | Sharp |
| Game bidding | Missed 1 game | OK | Missed 2 | Missed 2 |
| Competitive | Weak (4 bottoms) | OK | OK | Excellent |

**Persistent weakness: missing clear games.** Board 13 (28 HCP, 5-3 ♥ fit
in 3♥) and board 6 (26 HCP, minor suits, in 3♦) are both hands where
game should be automatic. This has appeared in previous tournaments.

### Recommendations

1. **R-BID review: game-forcing sequences.** After 1♥/1♠ opening, responder
   with 13+ HCP and 3-card support MUST force to game. No exceptions.
   Currently S-BID-RESP skills may not emphasise this enough.

2. **Minor-suit NT awareness.** When both hands have 6-7 card minors and
   combined 25+ HCP, 3NT should be the default target. The minors provide
   tricks; NT provides the scoring bonus.

3. **File hypothesis: Smolen after Split Stayman.** Board 13 pattern (5-3
   major fit, GF values) is exactly what Smolen solves. H-10's Smolen
   component (3♥/3♠ after 2♣-2♦) addresses this for 1NT auctions. But
   for suit openings, the forcing 1NT + preference sequence needs drilling.

4. **Defence remains the foundation.** 12 tops with many coming from superior
   defence. Continue prioritising H-7 (defensive bidding effectiveness) and
   lead discipline.
