- **Defence remains the engine** (71.9% / 8 boards). The win was built on
  defensive tops (B1, B13, B23) and partscore swings, not on bidding.
- **NS dominant, EW passive** — the +21.7pt seat gap is the largest yet.
  The EW seat both *missed games* (B5, B11, B14) and *leaked on defence*
  (B12). The NS seat was steadily strong but for one declarer slip (B17).
- **Conservative game/slam bidding**: four cold game/slam contracts left
  unbid. The bias is toward *underbidding*, consistent with the long-run
  record (H-29 game-threshold family; slam-ceiling pattern).
- **Solid competitive judgement when it counted**: the B24 4♥ sacrifice
  and the B1 punishment of NS's overbid show the table-feel is intact;
  the failures were positive actions not taken (games, slams, cashing a
  suit), not reckless ones taken.
