| NS | EW | Contract | Lead | Score | NS-MP | EW-MP |

**Our result: EW defending 1NT-1 → 0.0 MP (bottom)**

**Competitive bidding miss.** With 21 HCP EW and a 7-card spade fit,
the field reached 2♠= (+110) or pushed NS into 2NT-3 (+150). We
defended quietly, took the par 7 tricks against 1NT, but did not
compete.

**Auction**: NS opened 1NT (15-17 with S=15). Our E (Urs) held
♠AK754 ♥107 ♦Q73 ♣AJ4 — 14 HCP, 5-card spades. **Standard
balancing/overcall: 2♠ over 1NT** showing 5+ spades and a 10+ HCP
opener (or via Cappelletti/DONT). Urs passed.

**Why is this an H-7 (competitive bidding) miss, not a system gap?**
Because the system already supports 2♠ direct overcall of 1NT. The
selection — to balance the 5-card major in opener's NT auction — is
a judgement call that did not fire. **This is the same passive-EW
pattern that produced T11's B13 bottom** (13 HCP + 6♣ passed out vs
P2's 2♥). Two events with the same EW passivity.

**Suggested rule (informal, not yet hypothesis)**: with 14+ HCP and a
5-card major in direct seat over opponents' 1NT opening, overcall the
major rather than passing.

| NS | EW | Contract | Lead | Score | NS-MP | EW-MP |

**Our result: EW defending 3♠= → 0.0 MP (bottom)**

**Defensive 1-trick slip.** DD says 3♠ goes -1 (8 tricks max); pair 2
NS at +100 EW played 4♠ down 1, matching DD. Pair 6 NS at our table
played 3♠ and took 9 tricks (= DD+1). Pair 3 NS bid grand-slam (7♠X,
which is absurd at 23 HCP) and went -4 for the field-high +1100 EW.

Our defence let declarer take 9 tricks on a ♦A lead. **DD said 8.** The
extra trick came from a heart or spade tempo error.

**Could we have doubled?** No — at our table declarer made 9, so 3♠X+0
= +730 NS would have been catastrophic. Doubling would only work if
defence held to 8.

**Could we have bid 4♠ over 3♠ (sacrifice-or-push)?** With 17 HCP and
5-5 in diamonds and hearts, EW has 9D, 9H cold per DD. So 4♦ or 4♥ for
EW could push NS to 4♠ where the double pays. But at the table, after
1♠-pass-pass auction by us, balancing with 4♦/4♥ at unfavourable
vulnerability (EW V) is risky — DD double-checks 9 tricks but real
table play often loses one to a bad split.

**The cost was the 1-trick defensive slip combined with the field
seeing pair 3's grand-slam disaster.** Pair 5 EW just defended 7♠X and
got +1100 for free.
