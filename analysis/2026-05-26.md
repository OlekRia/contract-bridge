  Similar spread to T10 (32.0pt) — a mixed-to-weak field with one struggling pair.

  events (5-pt wins are typical). The depth of the field matters: P2 at 63.3%
  is a credible second.

  and rising.

  76.5% on 9 boards is normal for us; **declaring 68.8% on 16 boards is the
  highest declaring rate we have recorded.** T10 won with defence carrying a
  50% declaring rate; T11 won with both engines firing.

**Conclusion**: an outstanding club result and an internally meaningful shift
— we declared 64% of the boards (the highest share of any event) and still
beat top. The declarer ceiling that T10's analysis flagged as a weakness has
moved.

## Key Findings

### 1. Declarer-play step-change (the headline)
**16/25 = 64% of boards declared, 68.8% MP yield.** Previous events had
declared rates of 28-40% and 50-65% MP. T11 is the first event where we
both declared the majority of boards AND beat 65% as declarer. **Board 21
(3NT+2 vs others' -2)** is the signature: same contract, same opening
lead — the play divergence was the difference.

This is the kind of evidence that justifies promoting **H-16 (Expert
Dummy Analysis Protocol)** or similar declarer-training hypothesis from
active to validated. Recommend follow-up `/post-mortem` drill on B21 to
nail down what worked.

### 2. Wrong-strain pattern, 5th consecutive event
**B19**: 27 HCP balanced, chose 4♥ over 3NT, missed game by going down.
**B22**: 22 HCP balanced, chose 3♠ on a 5-2 fit instead of 2NT/3NT.

These are not auction errors of evaluation; they are **strain-choice
errors** with notrump available. Combined with T6/T8/T9/T10, we have
**13+ boards across 5 events** all telling the same story: when given a
choice between a minor/major fit (often 5-3) and NT, the system biases
to the suit. **H-28 (Strain Selection Protocol)** has decisive evidence.

### 3. NS asymmetry confirmed as a pattern (3 events)
- T8: NS 60.4% / EW 54.2% (+6.2pt)
- T10: NS 72.5% / EW 60.0% (+12.5pt)
- T11: NS 80.0% / EW 65.5% (+14.5pt)

Growing gap. Possible causes (none yet investigated): (a) our system bids
NS more profitably than EW (asymmetric agreements), (b) Ori-as-S is
stronger than Ori-as-W, (c) random clustering in 10-15 boards per event.
**H-30 (NS-EW asymmetry) is the right next step** — investigate root
cause across T8-T11 pooled data.

### 4. Game miss with 24 HCP + 9-card fit (2nd event)
**B7**: 24 HCP + 9-card ♥ fit, stopped in 3♥+1 = 170 instead of 4♥= 620.
Same as T10 B15. **H-29 (Lower game threshold with 9+ fit)** has corroboration.

### 5. Defensive containment is automatic now
B5 (+500 sacrifice double), B9 (W 2♥-3 = +300), B10 (W 6♠-1 = +100), B18
(holding E to 1♠=). Defence is no longer the differential — it's the
baseline. The series-9-event-average of 65%+ defending is the norm we
play to. **H-7 was correctly resolved; defence has become a moat.**

### 6. Penalty doubles down (1 vs 4)
T10 had 4 doubles for +2700; T11 had 1 (B5 +500). Opponents this event
were more cautious (P2 at 63% was a stronger #2 than T10's P4). Penalty
doubles are situation-dependent — not every event delivers them. The
strike rate when we do double remains ~100%.

### Immediate (before next tournament)

1. **File H-28 (Strain Selection Protocol)** — Director approval requested.
   The evidence base is now 5 events with 13+ boards. The protocol: with
