Splits: First half (boards 1-14) = +6 IMPs. Second half (boards 15-28) = +21 IMPs.

This is the **first team match in the 9-tournament series** (T1-T8 were all
matchpoint pair tournaments). Without the official Rangliste page (404), final
placement cannot be confirmed, but a +27 IMP differential across 28 boards
typically converts to a comfortable Win in head-to-head Swiss teams (≈ 14-15 VPs
on the WBF 0-20 scale).

### Why team scoring matters

Unlike matchpoints, where each board is worth at most 4 MP, a single team-IMP
swing can be 13 IMPs (slam vs game) or more. This raises the importance of
**bid discipline at game level** and **slam exploration** — both are now
amplified far beyond their MP cost.

## Conclusions

### What Went Well

1. **Slam bidding succeeded on board 10 (+13 IMP).** The system's slam machinery
   delivered 6S+1 with 38 HCP and 8-card fit. Best single-board result of the
   season so far in any tournament.
2. **Defence at our table generated +24 IMPs.** Boards 2, 6, 11, 25 all featured
   either setting a contract or holding declarer below DD. Continues the defence
   excellence trend from T8.
3. **Penalty double on board 18 (+12 IMP).** Found the X on opps' 4H and
   collected +500. Even though 4H was technically makeable DD, the double was
   the right MP/IMP play given the auction.
4. **Net team result +27 IMPs.** Comfortable win over 28 boards.

### What Went Wrong

1. **Partners' overbidding cost -32 IMPs (largest single category).** Six
   boards (3, 4, 12, 15, 16, 19) where partners NS pushed to game with marginal
   shape/HCP. This is a NEW pattern — partners' table contributions not
   previously visible in pair tournaments.
2. **Our heart overbidding repeated (boards 5, 13, -11 IMP).** Same as T8:
   bidding 4H with a 4-1 or 4-3 fit when 3NT was available (board 13). The
   strain-selection failure is the underlying issue — we're choosing the
   wrong denomination, not just the wrong level.
3. **Grand slam missed on board 10.** With 38 HCP + 8-card fit, 7S was makeable
   DD. Cost ~4 IMPs of additional gain. Slam exploration tools work for small
   slams but lack the next-tier discipline for grands.
4. **Slam missed on board 5.** With NS's 26 HCP and a 9-card diamond fit, 6D
   was cold. Neither table found it. Combined with our overbid of 4H by EW,
   this was a -5 IMP board that should have been +10+.

### Priority Actions

1. **PRIORITY 1: System review of NS auctions over 1m/1M openings.** Six boards
   of partners overbidding suggests the response framework pushes too aggressively
   to game on borderline hands. Investigate response ranges: are NS jumping to
   game when an invitational sequence (3-of-suit, 2NT inv) would work?
   Cross-check against H-22 (2/1 GF Continuations, ratified DEC-14) — could
   2/1 be auto-firing into game when partner's hand isn't extra-valued?
2. **PRIORITY 2: Fix our heart overbidding (T8 pattern continuing).** Boards 5
   and 13 repeated the T8 finding. **New angle: strain selection.** When EW
   has 24+ balanced HCP and a 4-3 major fit, prefer 3NT over 4-of-major. May
   need an explicit rule: do not raise responder's major suit to game with
   only 3-card support unless 5+ HCP extras.
3. **PRIORITY 3: Grand-slam exploration tool.** Board 10's grand was reachable
   with a king-ask after RKC. Investigate adding 5NT trump grand-slam force or
   5NT pick-a-slam to our slam framework. Pair with H-25's Acol 4NT specific
   ace ask (DEC-11) — extend the principle to grand-slam asks.
4. **PRIORITY 4: Maintain defensive excellence.** +24 IMPs from defence was
   the second-largest category. The defence work continues to pay off.
