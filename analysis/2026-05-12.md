### Field-Strength Assessment

The user asked "how does this rate against expert tournaments?". Honest read:

- **Field type**: 6-pair Tuesday club Howell, **32.0 percentage-point spread**
  between top and bottom (65.0% → 33.0%). Expert events typically compress to a
  10-15 pt spread; the wide gap here, especially the floor at 33.0% (P6 is 17pt
  below average), indicates a **mixed-to-weak field** with one struggling pair.
- **Margin of victory**: +13 pts is implausible at any expert event. In a
  Swiss-national pair final or ARC event, 58-62% wins; 65%+ winners are
  extremely rare. The size of our gap reflects field softness as much as our
  quality.
- **Calibration to expert level**: a portable estimate of our true MP-strength
  is closer to the **9-event ~62% average**. In an expert field (think Swiss
  Senior Pair, ARC, or a tough zonal qualifier), we would project to **56-60%**
  — competitive for the top half, podium-realistic but not dominant. Sustaining
  60%+ across stronger fields is the next test.
- **What this result _does_ prove**: defensive technique and penalty-double
  judgement are at a level that wins matches even when opponents are stronger.
  See the doubles haul below (4 boards, +2700 raw points from doubled penalties
  alone).

**Conclusion**: an outstanding club result, not yet evidence of expert-level
play. The 9 clear tops + 4 shared near-tops and 1 bottom are unusually clean
execution; the +300/+500/+800/+1100 penalty doubles are skills that travel. The
65% score in this field does not.

### H-28 (proposed): Strain Selection Protocol for Two 5-card Suits

**Statement**: When partnership has two 5-card suits with combined values
suggesting a partscore or game, prefer the major (especially spades) unless the
minor contains 3+ honors of A/K/Q rank concentrated in one hand.

**Falsifiable prediction**: Across 10 subsequent boards meeting the criterion,
spade contract scores will average ≥10 MP higher than the alternative-strain

### H-29 (proposed): 24+ HCP + 9-card fit downgrades game threshold

**Statement**: When the partnership has a 9+ card major fit and combined HCP
of 23-24, bid game (4-of-major) rather than stopping in 3.

**Falsifiable prediction**: B15 type hands score the field 5+ MP higher when
4-major is bid vs 3-major. Across 8 future boards meeting the criterion,
game-bid average MP ≥ 3.0 (top= 4.0).

**Justification**: B15 (T10) — entire field stopped in 2S+2 = 170 with 24 HCP
+ 9♠ fit. Game would have been 4S = 620, top by ~3 MP.

### H-30 (proposed): Re-validate NS-seat asymmetry

across pair tournaments (>5 percentage points across 100+ boards).

**Falsifiable prediction**: T11-T14 (next 4 events) NS-pct vs EW-pct gap will

gaps were sample noise).

**Justification**: T8 +6.2pt, T10 +12.5pt — two consecutive events showing
asymmetry after the T9 "resolved" finding.
