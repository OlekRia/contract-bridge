# ♠ ♥ ♦ ♣ All Fives ♣ ♦ ♥ ♠

> _A natural, disciplined bidding system built on common sense._

---

## Philosophy

- **No crazy openings.** Bids mean what they look like.
- **Partner is reliable.** Trust his bids.
- **Use the opponents' information.**
- **Stay flexible.** This is an experimental system.
- **Most auctions are competitive.** Keep opening principles in mind.

---

## Known Problems

| # | Problem |
|---|---------|
| 1 | 3rd/4th seat 1NT is 15–17 HCP (not 12–14), because a weak NT blocks us without a chance at game. After a 3rd-seat suit opening, the NT rebid shows 12–14 balanced. |
| 2 | The 4441 distribution. Every system struggles with this. |
| 3 | 1♠ usually promises 5 spades, but with a strong 4333 hand it may show only 4 — which feels awkward. |
| 4 | No agreed defense to opponents' 1NT opening yet. Currently we just pass. See hypothesis H-3. |

---

## The Central Idea

> **Every 1-level suit opening promises 5+ cards.**

With extra strength (15–17 balanced), opener may hold only 4 cards and escapes to NT on the next bid, or makes a reverse (16+ HCP).

> So while the opening _may_ show 4 cards, always think **5 by default**.

## Openings

### 1NT

**1st/2nd seat:** 12–14 HCP — shape 4333 / 4432 / 5332 (including a 5-card major).

**3rd seat:** 15–17 HCP — same shapes. Stronger range because with a weak 12–14 hand we risk blocking ourselves from game when partner passed with 11.

#### Responses to 1NT

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| 2♣ | **Puppet Stayman** — asks for a 5-card major |
| 2♦ | Transfer to ♥ |
| 2♥ | Transfer to ♠ |
| 2♠ | Transfer to ♣ |
| 3♣ | Transfer to ♦ |

Opener completes transfers normally. With maximum (13–14) and 4-card support, opener may super-accept by jumping to 3 of the major (see H-4).

#### Puppet Stayman Continuations

After 2♣, opener replies:

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| 2♦ | Denies 5-card major |
| 2♥ | 5 hearts |
| 2♠ | 5 spades |

After 2♣ — 2♦ (denial), responder continues:

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| 2♥ | Shows 4 **spades** (not hearts). Opener bids 2♠ with a fit, 2NT without. |
| 2♠ | Shows 4 **hearts** (not spades). Opener bids 3♥ with a fit, 2NT without. |
| 2NT | No 4-card major, invitational |
| 3NT | No 4-card major, game values |

> After the denial, responder's major bids are **transfer-like**: 2♥ shows spades, 2♠ shows hearts. This lets the strong hand (opener) become declarer.

#### Escaping 1NTx — Helvic Wriggle

After 1NT — (X):

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| XX | One-suited hand. Opener bids 2♣; responder passes or corrects. |
| 2♣ | ♣ + ♦ (touching pairs) |
| 2♦ | ♦ + ♥ (touching pairs) |
| 2♥ | ♥ + ♠ (touching pairs) |
| Pass | To play, or will bid later |

After Pass — Pass — **XX** = SOS with non-touching pairs (♣ + ♥ or ♦ + ♠). Opener bids 2♣; responder passes or corrects.

| Responder's Hand | Action |
|------------------|--------|
| Weak, shapely | Wriggle (escape to a suit fit) |
| Weak, balanced | Pass (hope for the best) |
| Strong (10+ HCP) | **Pass** — penalty. Thank them for doubling. |

> If opponents doubled, they show 15+ HCP. If responder has 10+, our side has 22+ combined — the opponents made a mistake. Pass and collect the penalty.

### 1♣ / 1♦ / 1♥ / 1♠

**1st/2nd seat:** 12+ HCP, 5+ cards (4+ with extra strength and NT escape).

- With two 4-card suits, **open the lower one first**.
- With 15–17 balanced, open 4 cards and **rebid NT**.
- A 2NT rebid confirms 4 cards (not 5) in the opening suit.

**3rd seat:** 8+ HCP, 5+ cards. This is a passed-hand opening — partner already passed, so there is no risk of missing game. The lighter range is tactical — competing for the partscore and directing the lead.

#### Continuations

Responder may raise with **3-card support** at the 2-level without fear — if opener had only 4, he simply rebids 2NT. Raises to 2NT and 3-level fits are **invitational**.

#### 1-Minor Opening, 1-Major Response

> **Key rule:** After 1♣ or 1♦, a response of 1♥ or 1♠ promises **5 cards** if weak. With invitational+ strength, may show only 4 (with a plan to rebid NT or raise). Therefore, **opener may support with just 3 cards!**

#### 1NT Response

6–9 HCP, no fit (at most 3 cards), no 5-card major, no game ambitions. We may miss a 4–4 fit, but if we are weak the opponents will bid. If not, everyone has misfit or balanced — NT is the right spot. See hypothesis H-2 for forcing 1NT as a future improvement.

#### Opener's Rebids After 1♣ — 1♥

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1♠ | 4+ spades, any strength (not a reverse — still at the 1-level) |
| 1NT | 15–17 HCP, balanced (confirms 4-card ♣ opening) |
| 2♣ | 6+ clubs, or 5 with extra strength |
| 2♦ | Reverse — 16+ HCP, 4+ ♦ (forcing) |
| 2♥ | Simple raise — 3+ ♥ support, not a reverse |
| 2♠ | Reverse — 16+ HCP, 4+ ♠ (forcing) |
| 2NT | 18–19 HCP, balanced (confirms 4-card ♣ opening) |
| 3♣ | 6+ clubs, invitational |

#### Quick Reference

| Sequence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 1♣ — 1♦ | Natural, 4+ diamonds, waiting |
| 1♣ — 1♥/♠ | 5+ if weak; 4+ if strong (invitational+) |

### 2♣ — Semi-Strong (Benji)

#### Type 1 — Balanced

**22–23 HCP:** 2♣ — 2♦ — 2NT

#### Type 2 — Strong Major (Acol-style)

2♣ — 2♦ — 2♥/♠

- About 5 losers
- At least 5 cards in the suit (usually 6+)
- At least 3 top honours (A, K, Q)
- Partner needs roughly 2 "fillers" to make game

#### Type 3 — Reserved

Under investigation for strong two-suiters (18–21, 5-5+). See hypothesis H-6.

#### Responses to 2♣

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2♦ | Waiting (0–7 HCP) |
| 2♥ | Positive relay — balanced, no 5-card suit, 8+ HCP |
| 2♠ | 5+ spades, 8+ HCP |
| 2NT | 5+ hearts, 8+ HCP |
| 3♣ / 3♦ | 5+ clubs/diamonds, game-force, slam interest opposite 22–23 |

All non-2♦ responses are effectively **game-forcing (8+ HCP)**. This avoids the problem where, after 2♣ — 2♦ — 2♥, responder cannot show a 5-card major.

> **Note:** 2♥ is NOT natural hearts — it is a general positive showing values without a 5-card suit. Hearts are shown via 2NT. The swap lets opener describe his hand at a low level.

### 2♦ — Strong Game-Force

Responder shows **controls** (A = 2, K = 1):

| Response | Controls |
|----------|----------|
| 2♥ | 0–1 |
| 2♠ | 2 |
| 2NT | 3 |
| 3♣ | 4 |
| 3♦ | 5 |
| 3♥ | 6 |

Excellent for slam exploration. Opener immediately knows the combined control count and can place the contract or ask further.

### 2♥ / 2♠ — Weak Two

**6–10 HCP, 6+ cards.** Disciplined, especially when vulnerable.

**Suit quality:** at least 2 of the top 5 honours (A, K, Q, J, T).

- 6+ cards in the bid major
- 6–10 HCP (not less, not more)
- Decent suit quality — partner may raise pre-emptively or try for game
- Avoid opening a weak two with a void or a strong 4-card side suit

| 3♦ | Minimum (6–7 HCP), good suit (2+ top honours) |
| 3♥ | Maximum (8–10 HCP), poor suit |
| 3♠ | Maximum (8–10 HCP), good suit |
| 3NT | Solid suit (AKQxxx+) — to play |

### 2NT — 20-21 HCP

Shape: 4333 / 4432 / 5332.

#### Responses to 2NT

| Bid | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| 3♣ | **Puppet Stayman** — asks for 5-card major |
| 3♦ | Transfer to ♥ |
| 3♥ | Transfer to ♠ |
| 4NT | Quantitative (inviting 6NT) |

After 3♣, opener replies: 3♦ = denies 5-card major (then check for 4-card fits), 3♥ = 5 hearts, 3♠ = 5 spades.

> Puppet Stayman fits better over 2NT than 1NT: the auction is already at the 3-level, so information leakage matters less, and finding the right major-suit game matters more.

## Competitive Bidding

### Overcalls

| Type | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Simple overcall | 8–16 HCP, 5+ cards. A good suit is more important than HCP. |
| Jump overcall | Weak — same as a weak two/three opening at the appropriate level. |
| Takeout double | Support for all unbid suits, typically 12+ HCP or any 17+. |

### RAPTOR Convention

An overcall of **1NT** over opponent's suit opening shows:

- **4 cards** in the unbid major
- **5+ cards** in a minor
- **10–16 HCP**

We bid using the **minimax principle** (aiming to sign off, tolerate a suit). This comes up more frequently than a natural 1NT (15–17) overcall. The HCP range is 10–16 because lighter hands pass and stronger hands start with a takeout double.

#### After Opponent Opens 1♥ or 1♠

Shows: 4 in unbid major + 5+ in a minor.

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Cue bid (opponent's suit) | Asks for the major |
| 2 of other minor | To play |
| 2 of unbid major | Natural, 5+ cards, willing to play |
| 2NT | Invitational+, asks overcaller's shape |

#### After Opponent Opens 1♣ or 1♦

Shows: 5+ in other minor + 4 in an unknown major.

| Response | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 2♣ (cheapest minor) | Pass or correct to overcaller's minor |
| Cue bid (opponent's suit) | Asks for the major |
| 2♥ / 2♠ | Natural, 5+ cards |
| 2NT | Invitational+, asks overcaller's shape |

## Defensive Carding

> **Use your head first, not partner's signals.** In most situations, the bidding and dummy tell you everything. Signals are a last resort.

### 1. Attitude (Encourage / Discourage)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Low** card | Encouraging — continue this suit |
| **High** card | Discouraging — please switch |

**Exception:** When partner leads the **A**, give **count** (even/odd) instead of attitude. If partner wants continuation from AK, he leads the K.

### 2. Count (Even / Odd)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **High** card | Even number of cards |
| **Low** card | Odd number of cards |

Easy to remember: from a **doubleton** (even), play **high**.

### 3. Suit Preference on Discards (Italian Style)

| Discard | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Odd** card (3, 5, 7, 9) | Interest **in the suit being discarded** |
| **Low even** card (2, 4) | Interest in the **lower-ranking** side suit |
| **High even** card (6, 8) | Interest in the **higher-ranking** side suit |

#### Signalling by Elimination

| Sequence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| 4 then 2 | Denies interest in the lower suit |
| 9 then 3 | Denies interest in the discarded suit |
| 8 then 6 | Denies interest in the higher suit |

> The higher the discard, the less reliable. The **second discard** confirms.

### 4. Opening Leads

**Lead 3rd or 5th highest from length.**

**Vs suits:** prefer top-of-sequence leads. Avoid leading from unsupported honours into declarer's strength.

**Vs NT:** lead your longest suit unless the bidding clearly shows opponents hold it.

#### From Length

| Holding | Lead | Rule |
|---------|------|------|
| K95 | **5** | 3rd highest |
| KQ752 | **2** | 5th highest |

#### From Sequences

| Holding | Lead |
|---------|------|
| AKx(x) | K (A asks for count) |
| KQx(x) | K |
| QJx(x) | Q |
| JTx(x) | J |
| T9x(x) | T |
| AKQx(x) | A (then K — showing the sequence) |

#### From Short Holdings

| Holding | Lead |
|---------|------|
| xx | Higher card |
| xxx | Middle, then high (MUD) |
| xxxx | Highest safe, then low |

> **Special rule:** If you hold the **J**, lead **4th highest**. Example: from KJ732, lead the **3** (not the 2).
