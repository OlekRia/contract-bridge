## Philosophy

We play solid and we play what we see. No wild bids, no destructive actions.
No gambles, no suicide, no guessing. Whatever partner bids, we take it at face
value and respect his choice.

- **No crazy openings.** We would never open 2έβξ with just 4 HCP έΑΦ that
  could ruin partner's decision-making. If partner knows you are reliable,
  he can trust that knowledge.
- **Bids mean what they look like.** A sequence like 1έβμ έΑΦ 3έβμ is not
  destructive έΑΦ it is an **invitation to 3NT**.
- **Use the opponents' information.** If they have shown 18+ points between
  both hands, temper your ambitions for game.
- **Stay flexible.** This is an experimental system and may be adjusted as
  we learn.
- **Don't obsess over uncontested auctions.** They are rare. Most of the
  time we face competitive bidding.

Keeping our opening principles in mind is essential, especially when the
auction gets contested.

## The Central Idea

> Maybe it is strange, but **we always open with 5+ cards in the suit.**

**1έβμ / 1έβο / 1έβξ / 1έβι** shows 5 cards with an unbalanced hand (long suit or
two-suited). This is what you should assume whenever you see a 1-level suit
opening.

**But with extra strength** (more than a bare minimum), opener may hold only
4 cards. In that case, opener "escapes" to NT on the next bid, or makes a
reverse (strong by definition).

> So while the opening _may_ show 4 cards, always think **5 by default**.

### Example: Opener's Rebids After 1έβμ έΑΦ 1έβξ

| Rebid | Meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| 1NT             | 15έΑΥ17 HCP (balanced escape) |
| 2NT             | 18έΑΥ19 HCP (balanced escape) |
| 2έβμ              | 6+ clubs, or 5 with extra strength |
| 2έβο / 2έβξ / 2έβι   | Reverse έΑΦ 16+ HCP |

**Bottom line:** if opener doesn't hold 5 cards, he holds exactly 4.

### 1NT έΑΦ 12-14 HCP

- 12έΑΥ14 HCP in 1st/2nd seat
- 15έΑΥ17 HCP in 3rd seat

**Shape:** 4333 / 4432 / 5332 (including a 5-card major έΑΦ yes, really).

Why 15έΑΥ17 in 3rd seat? With a weak 12έΑΥ14 hand we risk blocking ourselves
out of game. The stronger range keeps the door open.

| Bid | Meaning |
| --- | ------- |
| 2έβμ | **Puppet Stayman** έΑΦ asks for a 5-card major. 2έβο denies; then we check for 4-card fits. |
| 2έβο | Transfer to έβξ |
| 2έβξ | Transfer to έβι |
| 2έβι | Transfer to έβμ |

#### Escaping 1NTx (Helvic Wriggle)

After 1NT έΑΦ (X):

| Bid  | Meaning |
| ---- | ------- |
| XX   | One-suited hand. Opener bids 2έβμ; responder passes or corrects. |
| 2έβμ   | έβμ + έβο (touching pairs) |
| 2έβο   | έβο + έβξ (touching pairs) |
| 2έβξ   | έβξ + έβι (touching pairs) |
| Pass | To play, or will bid later |

After Pass έΑΦ Pass έΑΦ **XX** = SOS with non-touching pairs (έβμ + έβξ or έβο + έβι).
Opener bids 2έβμ; responder passes or corrects.

This finds a 7+ card fit almost every time. With a 12έΑΥ14 range we will get
doubled έΑΦ a reliable escape is essential, not optional.

**"But we lose Stayman and transfers!"** έΑΦ Yes, and that's fine. If the
opponents doubled, they are showing 15+ HCP. If responder is strong
(10+ HCP), our side has 22+ combined έΑΦ the opponents made a mistake.
**Pass and collect the penalty.** Playing 1NTx making with overtricks
scores far more than any game contract. You lose Stayman and transfers
precisely when you don't need them.

| Responder's Hand | Action |
| ---------------- | ------ |
| Weak, shapely    | Wriggle (escape to a suit fit) |
| Weak, balanced   | Pass (hope for the best) |
| Strong (10+ HCP) | **Pass** (penalty έΑΦ thank them for doubling) |

> **1NT is both the strength and the weakness of this system.** We may
> occasionally suffer, but in the long run we gain more than we lose έΑΦ
> especially by forcing the opponents to compete at a higher level from
> the start.

### 1-Suit Openings

**1έβμ / 1έβο / 1έβξ / 1έβι** έΑΦ 12+ HCP, 5+ cards (technically 4+ with escape)

- With two 4-card suits, **open the lower one first**.
- With a balanced 15έΑΥ17 HCP hand, open 4 cards and **rebid notrump**.
- 3rd seat: 8+ HCP with the same escape logic.

#### Continuations

Responder may bid a **3-card suit** fit at the 2-level without fear έΑΦ if
opener had only 4 cards, he simply rebids 2NT. So a 2NT rebid confirms
4 cards (not 5) in the opening suit.

**Example:** 1έβξ έΑΦ 2έβξ with 3-card support is perfectly standard.

> **Matchpoints:** Be cautious with 2-of-a-minor fits έΑΦ sometimes 1NT
> scores better.

Raises to **2NT** and **3-level** fits are **invitational**.

#### 1-Minor Opening, 1-Major Response

> **Key rule:** After 1έβμ or 1έβο, a response of 1έβξ or 1έβι promises **5 cards**
> if weak. With invitational+ strength, it may show only 4 cards (with a plan
> to rebid NT or raise). Therefore, **opener may support with just 3 cards!**

#### 1NT Response (Special Case)

A 1NT response typically shows:

- No fit for opener (at most 3 cards)
- **6έΑΥ9 HCP**
- No 5-card major to bid, and no game ambitions

We may miss a 4έΑΥ4 fit, but if we are weak, the opponents will bid again.
If not, everyone has a misfit or balanced hands έΑΦ and NT is the right spot.
Nobody panics about missing a 4έΑΥ4 fit when they open 15έΑΥ17. NT and all pass.

## Competitive Bidding

Generally standard methods apply. One key convention:

### RAPTOR Convention

An overcall of **1NT** shows:

- **4 cards** in the unbid major
- **5 cards** in a minor

We bid using the **minimax principle** (aiming to sign off, tolerate a suit).
This comes up more frequently than a natural 1NT (15έΑΥ17) overcall.

- If an opponent opens 1-Major έΑΦ we have 4 in the unbid major and 5 in a
  minor.
- If an opponent opens 1-Minor έΑΦ we show 5 cards in the other minor and 4
  cards in an unknown major.

#### Responses After Opponent Opens 1έβξ or 1έβι

| Response | Meaning |
| -------- | ------- |
| Cue bid (opponent's suit) | Asks for the major |
| 2 of other minor          | To play |
| 2 of unbid major          | Natural, 5+ cards, willing to play |
| 2NT                       | Invitational+, asks overcaller's shape |

#### Responses After Opponent Opens 1έβμ or 1έβο

| Response | Meaning |
| -------- | ------- |
| 2έβμ (cheapest minor)      | Pass or correct to overcaller's minor |
| Cue bid (opponent's suit) | Asks for the major |
| 2έβξ / 2έβι                   | Natural, 5+ cards |
| 2NT                       | Invitational+, asks overcaller's shape |

## Defensive Carding

> **Use your head first, not partner's signals.** In most situations, the
> bidding and the dummy tell you everything you need to know. Signals are a
> **last resort**.

Some players become obsessed with carding, stop thinking for themselves, and
put all their hopes on partner's signals έΑΦ that is a mistake.

**Use signals only at critical moments** έΑΦ typically at the start of play,
or when declarer runs a long suit and you must decide which suits to protect.

### 1. Attitude (Encourage / Discourage)

| Signal | Meaning |
| ------ | ------- |
| **Low** card  | Encouraging έΑΦ continue this suit |
| **High** card | Discouraging έΑΦ please switch |

**Exception:** When partner leads the **Ace**, give **count** (even/odd)
instead of attitude. If partner wants a continuation from AK, he leads the
King. If he leads A without K, he usually doesn't even see what you signal
and continues anyway. However, if an unexpected dummy appears, a correction
may be needed.

### 2. Count (Even / Odd)

| Signal | Meaning |
| ------ | ------- |
| **High** card | Even number of cards |
| **Low** card  | Odd number of cards |

Easy to remember: from a **doubleton** (even), you play **high**.

### 3. Suit Preference on Discards (Italian Style)

When discarding from a suit you don't need:

| Discard | Meaning |
| ------- | ------- |
| **Odd** card (3, 5, 7, 9)  | Interest **in the suit being discarded** |
| **Low even** card (2, 4)   | Interest in the **lower-ranking** side suit |
| **High even** card (6, 8)  | Interest in the **higher-ranking** side suit |

#### Signalling by Elimination

Sometimes you can't signal directly, so you deny:

| Sequence  | Meaning |
| --------- | ------- |
| 4 then 2  | Denies interest in the lower suit |
| 9 then 3  | Denies interest in the discarded suit |
| 8 then 6  | Denies interest in the higher suit |

> **Rule of thumb:** The higher the discard, the less reliable the signal.
> The **second discard** confirms.

### 4. Opening Leads

**Lead 3rd or 5th highest:**

| Holding | Lead  | Rule |
| ------- | ----- | ---- |
| K95     | **5** | 3rd highest |
| KQ752   | **2** | 5th highest |

#### Standard Lead Conventions

| Holding | Lead |
| ------- | ---- |
| xx      | Higher card |
| xxx     | Middle, then high (MUD) |
| xxxx    | Highest safe, then low |

> **Special rule:** If you hold the **J**, lead **4th highest**.
> Example: from KJ732, lead the **3** (not the 2).
