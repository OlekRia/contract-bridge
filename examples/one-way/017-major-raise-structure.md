# Major Raise Structure: 2NT / 3M / 4M (1st/2nd Seat)

Convention: After a 1-Major opening in 1st/2nd seat, responder with 4+ trumps
and GF values has three raises, each with a different slam message.

## Quick Reference

| Response | Meaning | Continuation |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| 2NT | LTC weak-suit ask (13+, 4+ trumps) | Opener shows suit with 3 losers |
| 3M | Slam try (13+, 4+ trumps) | Cue-bidding begins |
| 4M | Game, no slam interest | Pass (unless opener has 3 extra covers) |

### Opener's Replies to 2NT (LTC Weak-Suit Ask)

| Rebid | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 3C | 3 losers in clubs (xxx or Qxx) |
| 3D | 3 losers in diamonds (xxx or Qxx) |
| 3H (after 1S) | 3 losers in hearts (xxx or Qxx) |
| 3M (own suit) | No side suit with 3 losers -- all covered |
| 3S (after 1H) | 3 losers in spades (xxx or Qxx) |

## Hand 1 -- 2NT Ask: Honours Fill the Gap --> Slam

  South (opener): S A Q 8 5 3   H K J 4     D A 10 7   C 6 2
  North (you):    S K J 7 4     H A 5       D 8 3      C A K Q 5

  North: 17 HCP, 4 spades, GF. Want to know where opener's losers are.

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  Your response?

> 2NT (LTC weak-suit ask -- 4+ spades, GF, asking where opener's 3 losers are)

  South rebids: 3C (3 losers in clubs -- C62 = xxx type)

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 2NT   | Pass |
  | 3C    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  Your action?

> You hold AKQ in clubs -- PERFECT cover for opener's weakness!
> Combined LTC: opener ~6 losers, you ~5 losers. 24 - 11 = 13 tricks.
> Bid 4NT (RKCB) to check for aces, then bid 6S.

  Final contract: 6S

  Why 2NT worked: You learned that opener's only weakness is clubs.
  Your CAKQ5 fills the gap completely. Without the ask, you'd be
  guessing whether slam is right.

## Hand 2 -- 2NT Ask: Weakness Unfillable --> Stop at Game

  South (opener): S K Q 9 7 4   H A 8 3     D Q 5 2    C 7 4
  North (you):    S A J 6 3     H K Q 4     D 8 7 6    C A J 3

  North: 15 HCP, 4 spades, GF. Ask about losers.

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 2NT   | Pass |
  | 3C    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South shows 3 losers in clubs (C74).

> You hold CAJ3 -- only partial cover (AJ, but the 3 is a loser).
> More importantly, you also have D876 -- weak diamonds opposite DQ52.
> Neither hand stops diamonds solidly. Game is enough.
> Bid 4S.

  Final contract: 4S

  Why 2NT worked: You learned opener is weak in clubs but your own
  diamonds are also weak. Too many combined losers for slam.

## Hand 3 -- 2NT Ask: Opener Has No Weak Suit --> Slam Explore

  South (opener): S A K J 7 5   H A Q 3     D K J 4    C Q 8
  North (you):    S Q 10 6 4    H K 7 2     D A 5 3    C K 4 3

  North: 13 HCP, 4 spades, GF.

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 2NT   | Pass |
  | 3S    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South bids 3S = no side suit with 3 losers. All suits covered!

> Opener's hand is solid across the board. With 13 HCP opposite a
> hand with no weak spots, slam is likely. Cue-bid 4C (CK) or
> bid 4NT (RKCB).

  Final contract: 6S

## Hand 4 -- 3M Slam Try: Cue-Bidding Sequence

  South (opener): S A Q 10 7 3  H 6         D A K 8 4   C J 9 5
  North (you):    S K J 8 4     H A K 8 3   D Q 7       C A Q 2

  North: 18 HCP, 4 spades, slam interest. You want to explore via controls.

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  Your response?

> 3S (slam try -- 4+ spades, GF, slam interest, let's cue-bid)

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 3S    | Pass |
  | 4D    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South cue-bids 4D (DA or DK). Skipped clubs = no club control.

> You have CA -- covers the gap! Cue-bid 4H (HA).

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 3S    | Pass |
  | 4D    | Pass | 4H    | Pass |
  | 4NT   | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South bids 4NT (RKCB) -- enough controls confirmed.

> Respond to RKCB. Bid slam.

  Final contract: 6S

  Why 3M worked: Cue-bidding found that both hands have controls
  in each suit. The 2NT ask would have told you about losers, but
  3M lets you map out controls precisely for slam.

## Hand 5 -- 3M Slam Try: Controls Missing --> Stop

  South (opener): S K Q 8 6 5   H J 7 3     D K 9 4     C Q 6
  North (you):    S A J 7 4     H K 6       D A Q 5 3   C K 8 4

  North: 16 HCP, 4 spades, slam interest.

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 3S    | Pass |
  | 4D    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South cue-bids 4D (DK). Skipped clubs AND hearts = no control in either!

> You have HK and CK -- only kings, no aces. Opponents can cash HA
> and CA against a slam. Sign off 4S.

  Final contract: 4S

  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | ?     |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  Your response?

> 4S (game raise -- to play, no slam interest, shutout)

  South passes. Done.

## Hand 7 -- 4M Game Raise: Opener Has 3 Extra Covers

  South (opener): S A K Q J 7   H A K 3     D A 5 2    C 8 4
  North (you):    S 10 8 6 5 3  H 9 7 6     D 8        C A Q 5 3

  North: 7 HCP, 5 spades, distributional. Bids 4S (no slam interest).

  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | South | West | North | East |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  | 1S    | Pass | 4S    | Pass |
  | ?     |      |       |      |
  +-------+------+-------+------+
  South: AKQJ of trumps, two outside aces, DK. This is a monster --
  3 extra covers above minimum (3 aces beyond what responder expects).

> South bids 5C (cue-bid, slam try despite partner's sign-off).
> Responder with CA cooperates: 5H (no heart control? just raise).
> Responder: holds CA! Bid 5C... already bid by opener. Bid 5D (void!).
> South bids 6S.

  Final contract: 6S

  The 3-extra-covers rule: opener may override 4M sign-off when
  holding 3+ unexpected covers (aces, kings, or distributional
  features above a minimum opening).

## When to Use Which Raise

| Your hand | Bid | Reasoning |
|-----------|-----|-----------|
| 13+ HCP, 4+ trumps, want to check fit quality | 2NT | Ask where the losers are |
| 13+ HCP, 4+ trumps, have controls, want slam | 3M | Start cue-bidding |
| Any strength, 4+ trumps, game but no slam | 4M | Shutout / sign-off |
| 10-12 HCP, 3+ trumps, invitational | 1NT(f) then raise | Via forcing 1NT |
| 6-9 HCP, 3+ trumps | 2M | Simple raise |

### 2NT vs 3M Decision Guide

Both are GF with 4+ trumps and 13+ HCP. The choice depends on what you need:

- **2NT** when you have honours but don't know if they're WORKING.
  "I have AKx somewhere -- is it opposite your weakness?"
- **3M** when you have CONTROLS (aces, voids) and want to map them.
  "I have first-round controls -- do you have them too?"

Rule of thumb: **2NT asks about losers. 3M asks about controls.**
