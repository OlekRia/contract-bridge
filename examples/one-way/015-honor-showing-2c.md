# Honor-Showing Responses to 2C (H-27 Drill)

Convention: Replace blind 2D relay with honor-location responses.
Mnemonic: **DENY** — bid the major you DON'T have.

## Response Summary

| Bid | Meaning | Requirement |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| 2D  | Both major honors, 8+ HCP (positive) | A/K/Q in BOTH S and H |
| 2H  | Spade honor, deny hearts | A/K/Q in S, none in H |
| 2S  | Heart honor, deny spades | A/K/Q in H, none in S |
| 2NT | Bust — no major honor | No A/K/Q in either major |
| 3C  | Club A/K, no major honor, 5+ HCP | A or K in C (not just Q) |
| 3D  | Diamond A/K, no major honor, 5+ HCP | A or K in D (not just Q) |

Priority: major honor > minor honor > bust.
Both majors < 8 HCP: show spade honor (2H).

## Hand 2 — Type 2 Acol Spade + SpadeHonor (crossover deny)

  South (opener): ♠ A K J 10 8 5  ♡ A Q 3   ♢ K J     ♧ 7 2
  North (you):    ♠ Q 7 3         ♡ 8 5 4    ♢ A 8 7 4  ♧ 9 6 3

  North: 6 HCP, spade Q (honor!), no heart honor.
  Has spade honor, no heart honor.

  ┌───────┬──────┬───────┬──────┐
  │ South │ East │ North │ West │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2C    │ Pass │ ?     │      │
  └───────┴──────┴───────┴──────┘
  Your response?

> 2H

  2H — correct! "I have a spade honor, I DENY hearts."
  This is the crossover: 2H shows SPADE honor (deny the suit you bid).

  South hears 2H: partner has spade honor! With AKJT85, that's golden —
  the Q fills the gap perfectly.

  South rebids: 2S (Acol 2 spade — MATCH confirmed!)

  ┌───────┬──────┬───────┬──────┐
  │ South │ East │ North │ West │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2C    │ Pass │ 2H    │ Pass │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2S    │ Pass │ 4S    │ Pass │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ Pass  │ Pass │       │      │
  └───────┴──────┴───────┴──────┘
  Final: 4S. Same contract as blind relay, but opener KNOWS the Q is there.
  With better hands, this enables slam exploration.

  Key lesson: 2H = spade honor. The crossover saves a level — 2C-2H-2S
  is the most efficient match sequence possible.

  South rebids: 3C (Type 3 — clubs + another suit). South knows:
  partner has spade A! If the side suit is spades, that's a slam card.

  ┌───────┬──────┬───────┬──────┐
  │ South │ East │ North │ West │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2C    │ Pass │ 2H    │ Pass │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤

  North bids 3D (pass/correct). South corrects to 3S (clubs + spades).
  North: spade A + 3-card support = raise to game.

  Final: 4S. With blind relay, North might not know spades is the fit
  until a round later, potentially ending in 5C instead.

  Key lesson: Type 3 is where honor-showing shines. The spade honor

## Hand 6 — Type 3 DiamondsMajor + HeartHonor

  South (opener): ♠ A K 9 8 5  ♡ 3         ♢ A K Q 7 5  ♧ A 2
  North (you):    ♠ 7 4 3      ♡ K Q 8 5 2  ♢ 9 8       ♧ 8 7 5

  North: 5 HCP, no spade honor, heart K+Q (honors!).
  Has heart honor only (no spade honor).

  ┌───────┬──────┬───────┬──────┐
  │ South │ East │ North │ West │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2C    │ Pass │ ?     │      │
  └───────┴──────┴───────┴──────┘
  Your response?

> 2S

  2S — correct! Heart honor(s), deny spades.
  Crossover: 2S = I have HEART honor(s), NOT spades.

  South rebids: 3D (Type 3 — diamonds + a major).
  South knows: partner has heart honor but NO spade honor.
  If the major is hearts — mismatch (partner can't help spades).
  If the major is spades — the heart honor is the side suit.

  South's suits: diamonds + spades. Partner has heart honor = mismatch
  for the spade suit. But the diamond fit is huge.

  ┌───────┬──────┬───────┬──────┐
  │ South │ East │ North │ West │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 2C    │ Pass │ 2S    │ Pass │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤

  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ 3S    │ Pass │ 3NT   │ Pass │
  ├───────┼──────┼───────┼──────┤
  │ Pass  │ Pass │       │      │
  └───────┴──────┴───────┴──────┘
  North bids 3H (pass/correct). South corrects to 3S (diamonds + spades).
  North: no spade honor, 3 small = sign off 3NT.

  Final: 3NT. Opener reads the mismatch and doesn't push to 5D.

  Key lesson: Mismatch info prevents overbidding. Without honor-showing,
  opener might push to 5D hoping for spade help.
