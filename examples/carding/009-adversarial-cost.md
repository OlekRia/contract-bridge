# Adversarial Cost — When Declarer Reads Your Signal

## The Concept

Declarer sees your signals too. Before signaling, ask:

- **FREE signal**: Declarer already knows this information. Signal freely.
- **COSTLY signal**: Declarer doesn't know this. Your signal helps them.
  Only signal if the defensive gain clearly outweighs the declarer's gain.

---

## Deal 1: A FREE Signal (Declarer Already Knows)

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ Q 10 5
            ♥ 9 6 3
            ♦ K J 10 8 4
            ♣ A 2

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ J 8 7 4                   ♠ 9 3 2
♥ A Q 10 5                  ♥ K J 4 2
♦ 7 3                       ♦ 9 6 5
♣ 8 6 5                     ♣ J 10 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A K 6
            ♥ 8 7
            ♦ A Q 2
            ♣ K Q 9 7 4
```

Contract: **3NT by South**

South bid 1♣ – 1♦ – 2NT – 3NT.

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥5. Dummy ♥3. You play ♥2 (encouraging — ♥KJ42).

**Trick 2**: Declarer leads ♦2 to dummy's ♦K. You follow ♦5.

Now declarer plays ♦J from dummy. You follow ♦6. Partner follows ♦3.

Should you give count in diamonds? **YES — it's FREE.** Declarer bid
diamonds; they know their own length (3 cards). They can see dummy's
5 diamonds. They already know the split. Your count signal tells
partner something (your 3 diamonds), but tells declarer nothing new.

**Partner benefits**: knowing you have 3 diamonds tells partner that
declarer has 3 too. Partner knows to hold up the ♥A until trick 2 of
hearts (not relevant here, but the principle holds).

## Deal 2: A COSTLY Signal (Resolves Declarer's Guess)

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ A J 10 3
            ♥ K 9 4
            ♦ Q 7 6
            ♣ K 5 2

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 8 7 4                     ♠ K 9 5
♥ Q J 10 6                  ♥ 8 7 3
♦ A 5 3                     ♦ K 10 9 2
♣ 10 8 4                    ♣ J 6 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ Q 6 2
            ♥ A 5 2
            ♦ J 8 4
            ♣ A Q 9 7
```

Contract: **3NT by South**

Auction: 1♣ – Pass – 1♠ – Pass – 1NT – Pass – 3NT (all pass)

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥Q. Dummy ♥4. You play ♥3 (low = encouraging).
Declarer ducks.

**Trick 2**: Partner continues ♥J. Declarer wins ♥A.

**Trick 3**: Declarer leads ♠2 toward dummy's ♠AJ103.

Declarer's problem: finesse the ♠J (playing you for ♠K), or go up
♠A (hoping to drop a doubleton ♠K from partner)?

You hold ♠K95.

**DO NOT SIGNAL.** If you play ♠9 (high = discouraging / count),
declarer sees the ♠9 and thinks: "East played high — they might be
giving count from Kx or discouraging. But the 9 is conspicuous...
could they have the King?"

**PLAY SMOOTHLY: ♠5.** Don't help declarer. Play in tempo. The ♠5
is a neutral card — declarer still has a pure guess. If you play
♠9, you've turned a 50-50 guess into a giveaway.

---

## The Cost-Benefit Checklist

Before every signal, run this mental check:

| Question | If YES | If NO |
|---|---|---|
| Does declarer already know this? | FREE — signal away | COSTLY — think twice |
| Does partner need this urgently? | Signal even if costly | Don't signal |
| Does declarer have a guess here? | Stay silent | Signal is safer |
| Is the card I'd signal with free (SFS ≥ 2)? | OK to signal | Forced play — no choice |

---

## Common FREE Signals

1. **Count in declarer's bid suit** — they know their own length
2. **Attitude about partner's overcalled suit** — everyone heard it
3. **Suit preference when dummy's weakness is visible** — all see dummy
4. **Count late in the hand (trick 8+)** — distribution already known

## Common COSTLY Signals

1. **Count in an unbid suit** — reveals the split declarer can't see
2. **Attitude when declarer has a finesse guess** — resolves the guess
3. **Any signal in a suit where declarer is guessing honour placement**

---

## Key Takeaway

Defence is a partnership game played under adversarial conditions.
Declarer is the third reader of every signal you send. Ask yourself:
"Am I helping partner more than I'm helping declarer?" If the answer
is no, play smooth and let declarer guess.
