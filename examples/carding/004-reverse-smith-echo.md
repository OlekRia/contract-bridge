# Reverse Smith Echo — Attitude About the Opening Lead vs NT

## The Rule

vs NT only, trick 2+, when following to DECLARER's suit:

LOW = I LIKE the opening lead suit (continue it when you regain the lead).
HIGH = I DON'T LIKE the opening lead suit (switch when you get in).

"Reverse" because it follows our master direction: LOW = POSITIVE.
Standard Smith (used by some partnerships) is the opposite — we use
Reverse to keep LOW = ENCOURAGE consistent across all signal types.

---

## Deal

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ K 10 4
            ♥ Q J 5
            ♦ K Q J 8 3
            ♣ 9 2

West (You)                  East (Partner)
♠ Q J 9 7 3                 ♠ 8 6 2
♥ 8 6 4                     ♥ K 10 9 2
♦ 6 5                       ♦ 10 7 4
♣ K J 5                     ♣ A 8 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A 5
            ♥ A 7 3
            ♦ A 9 2
            ♣ Q 10 7 6 4
```

Contract: **3NT by South**

Auction: 1NT – 3NT (all pass)

---

## The Play

**Trick 1**: You (West) lead ♠7 (4th best from QJ973). Dummy plays
♠4, partner plays ♠2 (attitude: discouraging — only 862), declarer
wins ♠A.

Partner's ♠2 is low = encouraging? No — partner has ♠862, this is
their lowest. With only small cards, the "attitude" is ambiguous.
This is where Smith Echo helps on later tricks.

**Trick 2**: Declarer leads ♣4 to build the club suit.

Now partner must follow in clubs. They hold ♣A83. Partner knows:
if you get in, should you continue spades? Partner can see dummy's
♦KQJ83 threatening to run. The defence needs to establish spades
before declarer sets up clubs and diamonds.

**Partner's play: ♣3** (LOW = "I like your opening lead, continue
spades when you get in").

You win ♣K. You know partner wants spades continued. You play ♠Q,
driving out dummy's ♠K. When partner wins ♣A later, they return
a spade. Your ♠J93 cash for 3 spade tricks + ♣K + ♣A = down 1.

---

## What If Partner Didn't Like Spades?

Suppose partner held ♠862 ♥K1092 ♦1074 ♣A83 — same hand. But if
instead partner held ♠862 ♥A10 ♦10743 ♣A832 (♥A instead of ♥K),
they might prefer a heart switch.

In that case, partner plays ♣8 at trick 2 (HIGH = "don't like spades,
try something else"). When you win ♣K, you switch to hearts instead.

---

## When Reverse Smith Applies

1. Only vs NOTRUMP contracts (suit contracts use TSP / attitude)
2. Only when following to DECLARER's suit (not partner's, not dummy's)
3. Only after trick 1 (trick 1 uses attitude or obvious shift)
4. Only when you can afford the signal (SFS ≥ 2 in the suit you're
   following in)

---

## Adversarial Cost

Reverse Smith is COSTLY when declarer has a guess in the led suit.
If declarer is unsure whether to finesse your ♠Q or play partner for
it, your eager Smith signal might resolve the guess.

Reverse Smith is FREE when the led suit position is already known
(e.g., declarer took the ♠A at trick 1 — everyone knows you led
from a spade honour).

---

## Key Takeaway

Reverse Smith Echo is a cross-suit signal: you signal about the
opening lead suit while following in a different suit. LOW in
declarer's suit = "continue your lead." This is vital vs NT where
the race to establish suits decides the contract.
