# Parity Count Signal — Under Partner's Ace Lead

## The Rule

Highest EVEN spot = EVEN count. Highest ODD spot = ODD count.
Second card same parity: high→low = TRUE, low→high = FALSE.

Count is NOT a preference signal — it's pure information about length.
When partner leads the Ace, they need to know your count to plan the
defence. Do you have a doubleton (ruff coming) or three cards (no ruff)?

---

## Deal

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ K J 9 5
            ♥ 8 7 4
            ♦ Q J 10 3
            ♣ K 6

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 7 3                       ♠ 10 8 4 2
♥ A K 10 5 3                ♥ 6 2
♦ 8 7 4                     ♦ K 9 5
♣ 10 8 2                    ♣ Q 9 7 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A Q 6
            ♥ Q J 9
            ♦ A 6 2
            ♣ A J 5 4
```

Contract: **4♠ by South**

Auction: 1NT – 2♣ – 2♠ – 4♠ (all pass)

---

## The Play

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥A (Ace from AK).

The critical question for partner: should they cash ♥K and play a
third heart (hoping you ruff), or is there a better defence?

You hold ♥62 — a doubleton (even). Play highest EVEN spot.

**Your play: ♥6** (even spot = even count = doubleton).

**What happens next**:

**Trick 2**: Partner reads your ♥6 (even spot) as even count —
doubleton. They cash ♥K. You play ♥2 (also even — high→low,
same parity = TRUE even count confirmed).

**Trick 3**: Partner plays ♥3 (a low heart). Declarer plays ♥J,
dummy follows, and you RUFF with ♠2.

That's 3 tricks: ♥A, ♥K, heart ruff. Plus your ♦K = 4 tricks. Down 1.

---

## What If You Had Three Hearts?

If you held ♥632 instead, you'd play ♥3 at trick 1 (highest odd
spot = odd count = 3 cards). Partner knows a ruff isn't available
(you have 3 hearts, declarer has 3 hearts — no one ruffs on the
third round). Partner might switch to a diamond to build a trick
there instead of wasting the ♥K.

On trick 2, you'd play ♥2 (even — different parity from ♥3).
This is just a forced play, not a truth signal (truth check only
applies when both cards share the same parity).

---

## Truth Check in Action

If you wanted to DECEIVE declarer about your count (e.g., make
declarer think you have 3 hearts when you have 2):

Play ♥2 then ♥6 (low→high, both even = FALSE signal). Partner
reads ascending same-parity as false — knows your real count is
ODD, not even. But declarer sees two even cards and thinks "even."

When would you do this? Rarely under an Ace lead (partner needs
the truth). But in other count situations — when declarer is
reading your length to plan the play — the false signal is a
powerful deceptive tool.

---

## Key Takeaway

Under the Ace lead, ALWAYS give TRUE count. Partner needs to know
your length to decide: cash the King (setting up a ruff or cashing),
or switch (when hearts aren't going anywhere). The ♥6 from a
doubleton says "even" — partner can give you that ruff on round
three. The ♥2 on round two (high→low = TRUE) confirms it.
