# Attitude Signal — Basic Encourage / Discourage

## The Rule

LOW = ENCOURAGE (I have values, continue this suit).
HIGH = DISCOURAGE (nothing here, please switch).

This is the default signal — used whenever count and suit preference
are not specifically called for. Most common at trick 1.

---

## Deal

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ Q 10 8 3
            ♥ J 5 3
            ♦ K Q 9
            ♣ A 7 4

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 7 5 2                     ♠ J 4
♥ K Q 10 6                  ♥ A 7 2
♦ 8 5 3                     ♦ J 10 7 4 2
♣ Q 9 5                     ♣ 8 6 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A K 9 6
            ♥ 9 8 4
            ♦ A 6
            ♣ K J 10 2
```

Contract: **4♠ by South**

Auction: 1♣ – 1♠ – 3♠ – 4♠ (all pass)

---

## The Play

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥K (top of a sequence KQ10).

Dummy plays ♥3. You hold ♥A72.

**Your play: ♥2** (LOW = encouraging).

Why? You have the ♥A behind dummy. You want partner to continue
hearts. The ♥2 is the cheapest free card — never waste the ♥7
when the ♥2 sends the same message.

**What happens next**:

**Trick 2**: Partner sees ♥2 (encourage), continues ♥Q.
Declarer plays ♥8, dummy ♥5, you play ♥7.

**Trick 3**: Partner plays ♥10. Declarer ♥9, dummy ♥J, you win ♥A.

Three heart tricks cashed. Declarer loses 3 hearts + 0 spades = down 1
(or makes if you don't encourage and partner switches).

---

## The Wrong Defence

If you play ♥7 at trick 1 (high = discouraging), partner reads it as
"nothing in hearts, switch." Partner might try a diamond or a club,
and declarer draws trumps and pitches heart losers on ♦KQ.

---

## Key Takeaway

Attitude is binary: like or dislike. When you hold the Ace behind
dummy, you LIKE partner's lead. Say so with your lowest card.
The ♥2 shouts encouragement.
