# Dummy Type → Defence Line — Choosing Your Strategy

## The Concept

When dummy goes down, classify it into one of 8 types. Each type has
a primary defence line (from Five Lines of Defence). This mechanical
classification eliminates guesswork — the dummy tells you what to do.

### The 8 Dummy Types

| Type | Frequency | Primary Line | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| NT With Threat | 43.9% | Race to Cash | Long suit that can run |
| Stranded | 20.3% | Passive | Strong suit, no entries to reach it |
| Workshop | 16.1% | Attack Entries | Establishable suit + fragile entries |
| Mirror | 8.7% | Force Declarer | Balanced, mirrors declarer's shape |
| NT Balanced | 5.7% | Passive | Flat, no threats, no entries |
| Barren | 3.8% | Passive | Weak, short, nothing to establish |
| Powerhouse | 1.4% | Race to Cash | 16+ HCP, multiple threats |
| Ruffing | 0.1% | Cut Ruffs | Short side suit, trump entries |

### The 5 Defence Lines

1. **Race to Cash** — cash your tricks before declarer establishes theirs
2. **Attack Entries** — kill dummy's entry to a threatening suit
3. **Cut Ruffs** — lead trumps to prevent dummy from ruffing
4. **Force Declarer** — lead declarer's short suit to exhaust their trumps
5. **Passive** — don't help declarer; make them do the work

## Deal: Workshop Dummy → Attack Entries

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ 7 4 3
            ♥ K 5
            ♦ Q J 10 8 6
            ♣ 9 5 2

West (You)                  East (Partner)
♠ A 10 6                    ♠ K J 8 2
♥ Q J 10 8                  ♥ 9 7 4 2
♦ A 4                       ♦ K 3
♣ K J 7 3                   ♣ Q 10 8

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ Q 9 5
            ♥ A 6 3
            ♦ 9 7 5 2
            ♣ A 6 4
```

Contract: **2♦ by South** (competitive, pushed up)

---

## Step 1: Classify the Dummy

Dummy: ♠743 ♥K5 ♦QJ1086 ♣952.

- Long suit? ♦QJ1086 — 5 cards, QJ10 are establishable (not running,
  needs to drive out ♦A and ♦K).
- Entries? ♥K is the only potential entry. Fragile — one lead through
  it destroys it.
- Shape? Unbalanced (5-2-3-3).

**Dummy type: WORKSHOP** (establishable suit + fragile entry).

**Primary line: ATTACK ENTRIES.**

---

## Step 2: Execute the Defence

**Trick 1**: You lead ♥Q (top of sequence, attacking the ♥K entry).

Declarer wins ♥A (forced — can't duck, or ♥K is dead immediately).

**Trick 2**: Declarer leads ♦2 to establish diamonds. You duck ♦A
(let them think the ♦AK are split). Partner plays ♦3 (low = odd count
= 1 or 3 diamonds — here it's a doubleton, but the signal is ambiguous).

**Trick 3**: Dummy leads ♦Q. You win ♦A. Partner follows ♦K (!).

Partner unblocked the ♦K under your ♦A — excellent defence. Now
dummy's ♦J108 are established but...

**Trick 4**: You lead ♥J. The CRITICAL play. Dummy's ♥K is the ONLY
entry to the diamonds. Declarer must play ♥K now — but if they do,
they use the entry before the diamonds are fully established.

If declarer ducks ♥K, you continue hearts, destroying the entry
entirely.

Either way, dummy's ♦J1086 are stranded. The Workshop has no tools
to reach the bench.

---

## The Wrong Defence

If you lead a passive ♠ instead of attacking the ♥K entry, declarer
establishes diamonds at leisure: ♦ to ♦Q (ducked), ♦J (you win ♦A),
then declarer enters dummy via ♥K and cashes ♦1086. That's 4 diamond
tricks + ♥A + ♣A = contract made.

---

## Key Takeaway

Classify the dummy mechanically. Workshop = "they need to build AND
transport." Your job is to destroy the transport (entries). Lead
through dummy's honours, force out the entry before the suit is
established. The Five Lines framework turns vague defensive
instincts into concrete plans.
