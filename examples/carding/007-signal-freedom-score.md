# Signal Freedom Score — When You Can't Afford to Signal

## The Concept

Signal Freedom Score (SFS) = how many cards in your holding are FREE
for signaling (playing them cannot cost a trick).

The paradox: the BETTER your holding (AKQ, KQJ), the FEWER cards you
can spare for signals. The worst holdings (xxxxx) give maximum signal
freedom.

NEVER signal with a card that could win a trick.

## Deal 1: SFS = 2 (Binary Only)

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ K 9 7 4
            ♥ A J 6
            ♦ Q 10 8
            ♣ K 5 3

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 6 2                       ♠ A 10 3
♥ K Q 10 8 4                ♥ 9 5 2
♦ K 6 3                     ♦ J 7 5 4
♣ J 9 2                     ♣ Q 7 4

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ Q J 8 5
            ♥ 7 3
            ♦ A 9 2
            ♣ A 10 8 6
```

Contract: **4♠ by South**

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥K. Dummy plays ♥6.

You hold ♥952. SFS = 3 (all cards are free — no trick potential).
Plenty of signaling capacity.

**Your play: ♥9** (HIGH = discouraging — you have nothing in hearts).

## Deal 2: SFS = 1 (Forced Play — Silence Is the Signal)

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ Q 10 8
            ♥ J 5 3
            ♦ A K Q 6 3
            ♣ 7 4

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ J 5 3                     ♠ 9 7 6 2
♥ A K 9 7 6                 ♥ Q 4
♦ 10 4                      ♦ J 8 7
♣ K 5 2                     ♣ A 10 6 3

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A K 4
            ♥ 10 8 2
            ♦ 9 5 2
            ♣ Q J 9 8
```

Contract: **3NT by South**

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥7 (4th best from AK976). Dummy ♥3.

You hold ♥Q4. The ♥Q is a trick card — NEVER signal with it. Your
only free card is the ♥4.

SFS = 1. You are FORCED to play ♥4. No choice = no signal.

**Your play: ♥4** (forced — only free card).

**What partner infers**: Partner sees the ♥4 and thinks: "That's low.
Is it attitude (encourage) or is it forced?" Partner should consider:
- If you have Qx, the ♥4 is forced (SFS = 1)
- If you have 842, the ♥4 is encouraging (SFS = 3)
- Partner counts HCP: 8 in dummy + their own 13 = 21 visible.
  Declarer opened 1NT (12-14). So you have 40 − 21 − 14 = at least 5.
  The ♥Q is plausible.

**The silence inference**: your inability to signal IS the signal.
Partner plays ♥A at trick 2 (to pin your possible ♥Q). Your ♥Q drops.
Partner now cashes hearts.

## Deal 3: The Costly Signal Trap

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ A 10 7 3
            ♥ J 6 4
            ♦ K Q 9 2
            ♣ 8 5

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 8 4                       ♠ K J 5
♥ A K 9 5 2                 ♥ Q 8 3
♦ 10 6 5                    ♦ 8 7 4 3
♣ Q 10 3                    ♣ J 9 7

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ Q 9 6 2
            ♥ 10 7
            ♦ A J
            ♣ A K 6 4 2
```

Contract: **4♠ by South**

**Trick 1**: Partner leads ♥A. Dummy ♥4.

You hold ♥Q83. SFS = 2 (free cards: ♥8, ♥3). The ♥Q could be a
trick — never waste it as a signal.

**BAD play: ♥Q** (trying to "encourage"). This throws away a trick.
If partner plays ♥K next and leads a third heart, your ♥Q is gone.
You just turned a 3-trick holding (A, K, Q) into 2 tricks.

**GOOD play: ♥3** (low = odd count = 3 hearts). Partner knows you
have 3 hearts. They cash ♥K, then play a third heart. YOUR ♥Q WINS
the third round. Three defensive tricks from hearts alone.

## SFS Quick Reference

| SFS | Capacity | What You Can Do |
|-----|----------|-----------------|
| 0 | Nothing | All cards are trick-winners. Play in tempo. |
| 1 | Forced | Only 1 free card. No choice = partner must infer. |
| 2 | 1 bit | Binary signal only. Choose most valuable type. |
| 3 | ~1.6 bits | Good. High/mid/low over 1-2 tricks. |
| 4+ | 2+ bits | Full signaling luxury. |

---

## Key Takeaway

Before signaling, count your SFS. If you have AKx (SFS = 1), your
play is forced — partner must work it out. If you have Qxx (SFS = 2),
use the two small cards for one binary message. Never, ever signal
with a card that might win a trick. The cost is permanent.
