# Suit Preference on the Ruff Card

## The Concept

When you lead a card for partner to RUFF, the specific card you choose
carries suit preference:

- **HIGH card** = return the HIGHER side suit after ruffing
- **LOW card** = return the LOWER side suit after ruffing

This is not a new signal type — it's suit preference embedded in
your choice of card. You're going to lead the suit regardless; the
rank of the card is a free channel of information.

---

## Deal

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ K Q 10 7
            ♥ J 5 4
            ♦ Q J 6
            ♣ K 8 3

West (Partner)              East (You)
♠ 6 3                       ♠ 5
♥ A 8 7 2                   ♥ K 10 9 3
♦ 8 4                       ♦ A K 9 7 3
♣ J 10 9 6 2                ♣ Q 7 4

            South (Declarer)
            ♠ A J 9 8 4 2
            ♥ Q 6
            ♦ 10 5 2
            ♣ A 5
```

Contract: **4♠ by South**

Auction: 1♠ – 2♦ – 2♠ – Pass – 4♠ (all pass)

---

## The Play

**Trick 1**: You lead ♦A (top of AK in your overcalled suit).
Partner plays ♦8 (high = even count = doubleton). Declarer ♦2.

Partner has exactly 2 diamonds. One more diamond lead and partner
ruffs. But what should partner return AFTER the ruff?

You hold ♥K1093. If partner returns a heart, you win ♥K and cash ♥10.
Combined with partner's ♥A, that's devastating. Hearts is the HIGHER
side suit (♥ vs ♣, excluding trumps and diamonds).

**Trick 2**: You cash ♦K. Partner ♦4, declarer ♦5.

**Trick 3**: You lead ♦9 — **HIGH diamond = return the HIGHER suit
= hearts.**

Partner ruffs with ♠3. Partner reads the ♦9: "East wants hearts."

**Trick 4**: Partner leads ♥2. Dummy ♥4. You play ♥K. Declarer ♥6.

**Trick 5**: You return ♥10. Declarer ♥Q. Partner wins ♥A.

**Defence: ♦A + ♦K + ruff + ♥K + ♥A = 5 tricks. Down 2.**

---

## The Wrong Card

If you lazily lead ♦3 (your lowest) for the ruff, partner reads:
"LOW = lower suit = clubs." Partner returns ♣J. Declarer wins ♣A,
draws trumps, and concedes just one heart trick later. Down 1 instead
of down 2 — a full trick thrown away by choosing the wrong card.

---

## When This Applies

Suit preference on ruffs works any time you lead a suit knowing
partner will ruff:
- Partner showed a doubleton (count signal) or void (bid/play)
- You're giving partner a ruff after winning a side suit

The card costs you nothing — you're leading the suit anyway. The
rank is pure information, with zero adversarial cost.

---

## Key Takeaway

Every card is a message. When partner is going to ruff, you choose
WHICH card to lead. High = "come back in the higher suit." Low =
"come back in the lower suit." It's free, it's clear, and here it
was worth an extra trick.
