# Tempo Read — Placing Cards from Opponent Hesitation

## The Concept

When an opponent hesitates before passing or bidding, they reveal
borderline values. A long pause before passing your 1NT says "I had
something to say." You may legally infer from *opponents'* tempo
(never partner's). This changes how you play the hand.

---

## Deal

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ 10 6 2
            ♥ A K 8 4
            ♦ A Q 9 4
            ♣ J 5

West                        East
♠ A Q J 8 5                 ♠ 7 4
♥ J 6 3                     ♥ 10 9 5
♦ K 7 3                     ♦ J 10 8
♣ Q 8                       ♣ 10 9 7 4 2

            South (You)
            ♠ K 9 3
            ♥ Q 7 2
            ♦ 6 5 2
            ♣ A K 6 3
```

North: 14 HCP. East: 1 HCP. South: 12 HCP. West: 13 HCP. Total: 40.

Contract: **3NT by South**

Auction: 1NT – *[West hesitates 8 seconds, passes]* – 2♣ – Pass –
2♦ – Pass – 3NT – all pass.

North has 14 HCP and 4 hearts — Stayman to check for a 4-4 heart
fit. South's 2♦ denies a 4-card major (♠K93, ♥Q72). North bids 3NT.

---

## The Observation

West thought for 8 seconds before passing over 1NT. At club level,
this means 11-14 HCP — enough to consider a 2♠ overcall with a
5-card suit, but ultimately passing.

**What you know before a card is played:**

- West has ~11-14 HCP (hesitation)
- East has very little (40 − 12 − 14 − West's ~13 ≈ 1)

---

## The Play

**Trick 1**: West leads ♠8 (4th best from ♠AQJ85). Rule of 11:
11 − 8 = 3 cards above ♠8 in the other hands. Dummy has ♠10 (1).
You have ♠K, ♠9 (2). Total 3 — East has zero cards above ♠8. Dummy
plays ♠2, East ♠4, you win ♠9.

**Trick 2**: You lead ♣3 to dummy's ♣J (won).

**Trick 3**: Dummy ♣5 back. East ♣2. You win ♣A.

**Trick 4**: You cash ♣K. West ♣Q, dummy discards ♥4.

You have 4 tricks (♠9, ♣J, ♣AK). Need 5 more from 9 remaining cards.

**Your position**: ♠K3, ♥Q72, ♦652. Dummy: ♠T6, ♥AK8, ♦AQ94.

**Safe tricks**: ♥AKQ = 3. Total so far: 4 + 3 = 7. Need 2 more
from diamonds.

Diamonds: ♦652 opposite ♦AQ94. The critical finesse:
- Lead ♦2 from hand, finesse ♦Q in dummy: works if West has ♦K
- Cash ♦A first (playing for the drop): works only if ♦K is
  doubleton — unlikely with 3-3-3 splits

**The tempo read resolves this**: West hesitated with ~13 HCP.
You've seen ♠AQJ = 7 HCP from the opening lead (4th best from a
suit headed by AQJ). West also played ♣Q = 2 HCP. That's 9 HCP
visible.

West needs ~4 more HCP for the hesitation range (11-14):
- ♦K (3 HCP) + ♥J (1 HCP) = 4 more. Total 13 HCP. Perfect for
  an 8-second hesitation — strong enough to think about 2♠ with
  AQJxx and 13 HCP, but not quite enough to act over 1NT.
- Without ♦K: West has only ♠AQJ(7) + ♣Q(2) + ♥J(1) = 10 HCP.
  10 HCP with a 5-card spade suit is a clear pass — no hesitation.

**Decision**: West has ♦K. Finesse through West.

**Trick 5**: You lead ♦2. West plays ♦3 (smooth). Dummy ♦Q.
East ♦8. **♦Q wins!**

**Trick 6**: Dummy ♥A.

**Trick 7**: Dummy ♥K.

**Trick 8**: Dummy ♥8 to your ♥Q.

**Trick 9**: You lead ♦5. West ♦7. Dummy ♦A. East ♦10.

9 tricks: ♠9, ♣JAK, ♥AKQ, ♦QA. **3NT made.**

---

## Without the Tempo Read

If you misplace ♦K with East, you might cash ♦A first (hoping ♦K
drops), then lead ♦9 toward your hand. East plays ♦J. You have ♦65.
West's ♦K7 beats whatever you play — you get only 1 diamond trick
(♦A). Total: 4 clubs + 3 hearts + 1 spade + 1 diamond = 8 tricks.
Down 1.

The wrong guess costs you the contract. The tempo read converted a
50-50 diamond guess into a near-certainty.

---

## Ethical Note

Using opponents' tempo is explicitly permitted by Law 73. You may
NOT act on partner's hesitations. The distinction is absolute:

- **Opponent pauses** → legal information, use it
- **Partner pauses** → unauthorized information, ignore it

---

## Key Takeaway

An 8-second hesitation over your 1NT places 11-14 HCP with the
thinker. As the play develops and you see their high cards, subtract
from the estimated range. When you reach a critical finesse, the
remaining HCP gap tells you where the missing honour sits. The
tempo read converted a 50-50 diamond guess into a near-certainty.
