# Half-a-Loaf — Best Result Possible, Not Best Possible Result

## The Concept

Aim for the best result POSSIBLE, not the best POSSIBLE result. A
secure plus score is better than a speculative slam. An overtrick
you can't lose is better than two overtricks that risk the contract.
Even a quarter of potential points is better than zero.

Herbert Simon's satisficing principle applied to bridge: "good enough"
beats "optimal but risky."

---

## Deal 1: Secure the Contract, Forget the Overtrick

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ A 9 5 3
            ♥ K 8 4
            ♦ Q J 10 6
            ♣ 7 3

West                        East
♠ J 10 8 4                  ♠ 7 2
♥ Q J 10 9                  ♥ 7 6 5 3
♦ 7 3                       ♦ K 9 8 4
♣ K 6 2                     ♣ Q 10 4

            South (You)
            ♠ K Q 6
            ♥ A 2
            ♦ A 5 2
            ♣ A J 9 8 5
```

Contract: **3NT by South**

Auction: 1♣ – Pass – 1♠ – Pass – 2NT – Pass – 3NT – All Pass

---

## The Greedy Line (Wrong)

**Trick 1**: West leads ♥Q. You win ♥A.

You count tricks: ♠AKQ (3), ♥A (1), ♦A (1) = 5 certain. Need 4 more
from clubs or diamonds.

**Greedy plan**: Finesse ♣J to try for 5 club tricks.

**Trick 2**: You lead ♣5. West ♣2. Dummy ♣3... wait, dummy only has
♣73. You can't finesse from dummy.

OK — lead ♣8 from hand. West ♣6. Dummy ♣7 (forced). East ♣Q.
**East wins ♣Q.**

East returns ♥5. West's ♥J109 run. That's 4 heart tricks for the
defence + ♣Q already won = down 1.

You gambled on the club finesse for an extra trick, and the contract
went down.

---

## The Half-a-Loaf Line (Right)

**Trick 1**: West leads ♥Q. You win ♥A.

**Count safely**: ♠AKQ (3), ♥AK (2), ♦A (1) = 6 top tricks.
Diamonds can provide 3 more if you drive out ♦K: ♦QJ10 from dummy
after ♦A from hand = 3 diamond tricks once ♦K is knocked out.

6 + 3 = 9 tricks. **You don't need clubs at all.**

**Trick 2**: Lead ♦2 to dummy's ♦Q. East plays ♦4.

**Trick 3**: Dummy ♦J. East wins ♦K. West ♦3.

**Trick 4**: East returns ♥3. West ♥9. Dummy ♥K. Hearts are 4-4
(West has ♥J10 left, but you've knocked out ♦K and have ♦A10 set up).

**Trick 5**: Dummy ♦10 (good). East ♦8. You pitch ♣5. West ♦7.

**Trick 6**: Dummy ♦6 (good). You pitch ♣8.

You now have: ♠AKQ (3), ♥AK (already played 2), ♦ tricks (3) = 9.
Contract made. No risk.

## Deal 2: Take the Sure Plus

```
            North (Dummy)
            ♠ K 8 7 5
            ♥ A J 9 3
            ♦ K 4
            ♣ 10 6 5

West                        East
♠ 3                         ♠ Q J 10 6 4
♥ K 10 7 4                  ♥ 8 5
♦ Q J 10 7 6                ♦ 9 8 3
♣ A 9 3                     ♣ K 8 4

            South (You)
            ♠ A 9 2
            ♥ Q 6 2
            ♦ A 5 2
            ♣ Q J 7 2
```

Auction: 1♣ – 1♦ – 1♥ – 1♠ – 1NT – Pass – Pass – ?

You're in 1NT. You have a heart fit with partner (4-3). Should you
bid 2♥ to compete? You have 12 HCP, partner responded.

**Half-a-Loaf says: PASS.** You're already in 1NT. If you can
scramble 7 tricks, that's a plus score. 2♥ might go down when 1NT
makes. Don't chase the "better" contract when the current one is
adequate.

**Result**: 1NT makes exactly. ♠AK (2), ♥AQ (2, finesse loses),
♦AK (2), ♣Q (1, if clubs split) = 7 tricks. +90 is better than
2♥-1 = -50.

## The Half-a-Loaf Checklist

| Situation | Greedy Line | Half-a-Loaf Line |
|---|---|---|
| 3NT with 9 tricks available safely | Finesse for overtricks | Cash 9 tricks, go home |
| Partscore that makes | Compete to game zone | Accept the plus score |
| 4♠ with an overtrick via risky finesse | Take the finesse | Claim 10 tricks |
| Slam with a safety play available | Play for 13 tricks | Take the safety play |
| Competitive auction at the 3-level | Push to 4 | Defend their 3-level bid |

---

## When NOT to Apply Half-a-Loaf

- **Teams scoring (IMPs)**: the extra tricks matter less — secure the contract
- **Matchpoints when the field is in the same contract**: overtricks matter.
  But even at matchpoints, going down costs more than the overtrick gains.
  An average (making the contract) beats a bottom (going down).
- **Slam vs game**: the slam bonus is so large that it justifies some risk.
  But even then, prefer the safety play if it exists.

---

## Key Takeaway

Every hand has a safe line and a greedy line. The greedy line gains
an overtrick when it works but loses the whole contract when it
doesn't. The safe line gives up a potential overtrick but guarantees
the contract. Over 100 boards, the safe line wins more total points.
Aim for the best result POSSIBLE — the one you can actually achieve.
