## Board 6 — ELITE Defence: Set 4♥ When DD Says It Makes | 8.0 MP

**Round 3, we are NS.** East declared.

```
              ♠ 9853         ← Our North
              ♥ 84
              ♦ 8643
              ♣ J105
♠ A10                 ♠ QJ42
♥ AJ                  ♥ K10976   ← Declarer (East)
♦ K10975              ♦ Q
♣ A732                ♣ Q94
              ♠ K76        ← Our South
              ♥ Q532
              ♦ AJ2
              ♣ K86
```

**Contract:** E 4♥. DD: 10 tricks. **Result:** 4♥-1 = +100 (TOP — only table to set it).

### Bidding Reconstruction (DEC-8, F-DEF-015)

**Pre-dummy (South's perspective):**
- South opened 1♦ (15 HCP, 5-card diamond rule — actually South has 13 HCP; the
  opening may have been by West). Regardless, EW reached 4♥ via a takeout double
  and raise.
- Hearts is the agreed suit → 8+ hearts between EW expected.
- South holds ♥Q532 — the ♥Q sitting over dummy could be valuable.

**Post-dummy (after West tables as dummy):**
- West: ♠A10, ♥AJ, ♦K10975, ♣A732 → 15 HCP, 2 hearts only
- East therefore: ~12 HCP with 5+ hearts (responded to double with 2♥, raised to game)
- Combined EW: ~27 HCP → NS has ~13 HCP
- South: 13 HCP → North: ~0 HCP (confirmed by ♠9853 ♥84 ♦8643 ♣J105)

### Dummy Suit Taxonomy (F-DEF-014)

South sees dummy (West):
- ♠A10: **Entry suit** — ♠A is a sure entry; ♠10 offers no running threat
- ♥AJ: **Trump honour** — only 2 trumps but ♥AJ doubleton is powerful; declarer
  can finesse through South's ♥Q if he reads the position
- ♦K10975: **Running threat** — 5 diamonds with ♦K; if declarer can draw trumps
  and reach dummy, diamonds provide discards
- ♣A732: **Entry suit** — ♣A is a sure entry; ♣732 is passive

### Defence Line Selection (DEC-8, Five Lines)

**Critical assessment:** Dummy's ♦K10975 is the main threat — if declarer draws
trumps and runs diamonds, our side-suit winners evaporate.

**Line chosen: RACE TO CASH + ATTACK TIMING.**

South's defensive assets: ♥Q (trump trick if timed right), ♦AJ (behind dummy's
♦K10975 — a natural stopper), ♣K (trick behind ♣A).

1. South must NOT lead diamonds — that helps declarer establish the diamond suit
2. Cash/establish side tricks BEFORE declarer can draw trumps and run diamonds
3. ♥Q532 behind declarer's ♥K10976: if declarer finesses toward dummy's ♥AJ,
   South's ♥Q is onside for declarer — but if we force declarer to play trumps
   from hand (♥K), our ♥Q scores in the wash

### Opening Lead Analysis

South's hand: ♠K76 ♥Q532 ♦AJ2 ♣K86. Against 4♥:

- ♠K: Aggressive, attacking dummy's ♠A10. Risks giving away a trick.
- ♦A: Active — sees dummy's diamonds and decides continuation. But ♦A lead
  helps declarer establish ♦K10975.
- ♣8: Passive, 4th-best. Gives nothing away. Declarer has to do the work.
- Trump: Never lead from ♥Qxxx into a heart contract.

**Best lead: ♣8** (passive, small from three). Forces declarer to manage entries
and trumps without help. The diamonds are the threat suit — don't touch them.

### Signal Freedom Score — Key Holdings

**South's ♥Q532:**
- SFS = 2 (♥5 and ♥2 are free; ♥Q and ♥3 border between trick/free)
- Signal capacity: ~1 bit
- When forced to follow in trumps, South plays ♥2 first (low = no preference
  between side suits) or uses TSP: ♥5 first = prefer higher suit (spades),
  ♥2 first = prefer lower suit (diamonds/clubs)

**North's ♠9853:**
- SFS = 4 (all cards free — no trick potential)
- Signal capacity: 2+ bits
- North can signal freely on any suit: attitude, count, or suit preference.
  ♠9 = discouraging (reversed attitude: high = discourage); ♠3 = encouraging (low = encourage). Note: Italian SP applies only to discards, not follow-suit

**South's ♦AJ2:**
- SFS = 1 (♦2 is the only free card; ♦A and ♦J are trick cards)
- **Silence Inference (F-DEF-018):** South cannot signal in diamonds without
  costing a trick. If diamonds are led, South is forced to play trick cards.
  Partner should infer diamond honours from South's inability to signal.

**South's ♣K86:**
- SFS = 2 (♣8 and ♣6 free; ♣K is a trick card)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit

### Critical Defensive Sequence

The defence that beat DD required:
1. **Passive lead** (not diamonds) — force declarer to handle entries alone
2. **♦AJ behind ♦K10975:** When declarer attacks diamonds, South's ♦AJ stops
   the run. Declarer needs two diamond entries to establish the suit, but
   South's ♦J controls the second round.
3. **♥Q scores:** With careful timing, South's ♥Q takes a trump trick —
   whether by declarer misguessing or by the defensive forcing play
4. **Four tricks:** ♥Q + ♦A + ♣K + one more (♦J or a spade trick via ♠K)

### Obvious Shift Analysis (DEC-7 §1, R-DEF-015)

At trick 1, after seeing dummy's ♦K10975 (running threat) and ♣A732 (entry):
- **Obvious shift suit = diamonds** (the suit where dummy threatens to establish
  winners). If partner leads a neutral suit and you win, the obvious shift is
  to NOT play diamonds — attacking diamonds helps declarer.
- Partner's attitude signal at trick 1 refers to the obvious shift suit
  (diamonds), not the suit led.

### Impact

Every other table made 4♥ (two tables: +170, one: -620, one: -790). Only at our
table did 4♥ go down. South's ♦AJ behind dummy's ♦K10975 was the critical
defensive holding — preventing declarer from establishing diamond winners. The
♥Q532 provided the trump trick that tipped the balance.

Defence plan: Cash ♦AK, then exit passively. ♥K74 produces a natural trump trick.
Need to find a 4th trick from spades (♠A) or clubs (♣K).

West holds ♠10973, ♥102, ♦J653, ♣764. Against South's 4♥ after partner doubled:

- ♦5: Partner doubled — likely has diamond values. Lead into partner's strength.
  3rd-highest from even length (DEC-7 §1). East sees ♦5 and applies Rule of 11:
  11 - 5 = 6 cards above the 5 outside West → confirms diamond honours.

**3rd/5th lead: ♦5** (3rd from 4, even length). Partner's double promised values;

### Signal Freedom Score — Critical Analysis

**East's ♥K74:**

**West's ♠10973:**

**East's ♦AK874:**

**East's ♣K5:**

1. **Trick 1:** West leads ♦5 (3rd from ♦J653). Dummy plays ♦2, East wins ♦A.
2. **Trick 2:** East cashes ♦K. Declarer ruffs (singleton ♦9).

The key: East's ♦AK were cashed BEFORE declarer could discard on spades. Timing

> **Compass note:** Boardzettel places our South hand at South and the ♠K854 hand

> consistent with the scored contract: West = declarer.

**Contract:** W 3NT. DD: 9 tricks. **Result:** 3NT-1 = +50 (GOOD — 6.0 MP).

### Bidding Reconstruction (DEC-8, F-DEF-015)

**Pre-dummy (South's perspective):**
- West opened or East responded NT. Combined EW: ~24-26 HCP.
- South holds 14 HCP (♠Q6, ♥Q5, ♦AJ1063, ♣A973) — strong defensive hand.

**Post-dummy (after East tables):**
- East: ♠J32, ♥KJ32, ♦Q95, ♣KQ6 → 13 HCP
- West therefore: ~12-14 HCP
- Combined EW: ~25-27 HCP → NS has ~13-15 HCP
- South: 14 HCP → North: ~4 HCP (♠A1097 = 4 HCP, confirmed)

### Dummy Suit Taxonomy (F-DEF-014)

South sees dummy (East):
- ♠J32: **Attack target** — ♠J behind North's ♠A1097; the spade suit favours NS
- ♥KJ32: **Establishable source** — ♥KJ can produce tricks; South's ♥Q5 doubleton
  is a partial stopper
- ♦Q95: **Attack target** — South holds ♦AJ1063 directly behind ♦Q95
- ♣KQ6: **Running threat** — ♣KQ can produce fast tricks; South's ♣A controls once

### Opening Lead Analysis (R-DEF-001)

South holds ♠Q6, ♥Q5, ♦AJ1063, ♣A973. Against 3NT, lead 4th-best from longest suit.

**♦3** (4th-best from ♦AJ1063): 6-card suit, strong interior. Rule of 11: 11 - 3 = 8
cards above the ♦3 outside South. North knows South has length and the defence can
establish diamonds before declarer runs 9 tricks.

Under DEC-7 3rd/5th convention: from 6 cards (even length), 3rd-highest = ♦10.
But from AJ10xx, the ♦10 is a trick card. Standard lead: ♦3 (4th-best) or ♦6 (5th).

### Signal Freedom Score

**North's ♠A1097 on spade continuation:**
- SFS = 2 (♠9 and ♠7 are free; ♠A and ♠10 are trick cards)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- ♠7 = encouraging (reversed: low = encourage); ♠9 = discouraging (high = discourage)

**South's ♦AJ1063:**
- SFS = 2 (♦6 and ♦3 are free; ♦A, ♦J, ♦10 are trick cards)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- After winning a diamond, South's continuation is forced by the holding

**North's ♣J1052 on club plays:**
- SFS = 3 (♣10, ♣5, ♣2 are free; ♣J is border trick/free)
- Signal capacity: ~1.6 bits
- North can signal count or preference freely in clubs

### Reverse Smith Echo (DEC-7 §6, R-DEF-013)

**NT contract — Reverse Smith Echo applies at trick 2+.**

After the diamond lead, suppose declarer switches to establish hearts or clubs.
South gives a **Reverse Smith Echo** on the next suit led:

- South plays **LOW** in the new suit = "I like my opening lead suit (diamonds),
  please return it"
- South plays **HIGH** = "Switch, don't return diamonds"

With ♦AJ1063, South desperately wants diamonds continued — Reverse Smith LOW on any
non-diamond suit declarer plays. This is a FREE signal: it costs nothing because
the card order doesn't affect South's trick potential in the suit declarer is playing.

### Obvious Shift Analysis (DEC-7 §1, R-DEF-015)

At trick 1, after diamond lead and seeing dummy:
- **Obvious shift suit = spades** (♠J32 in dummy is the weakest holding; North's
  ♠A1097 sits behind it)
- If North wins a trick, the obvious shift is to spades — the suit where dummy
  is weakest and NS has the most to gain

### Critical Defensive Sequence

1. **Trick 1:** South leads ♦3 (4th-best). Dummy plays ♦5, North plays ♦2
   (count: low = odd), declarer wins ♦K.
2. **Declarer's problem:** Diamonds are established for the defence. Declarer needs
   to set up 9 tricks quickly from ♣KQ, ♥KJ, and ♠ entries.
3. **Defence holds up:** South's ♦AJ1063 controls the diamond suit. When declarer
   plays clubs or hearts, South gives Reverse Smith LOW on the first opportunity.
4. **Setting tricks:** ♦A + ♦J + ♦10 (established) + ♣A = 4 defensive tricks
   before declarer can run 9.

### Impact

DD says 3NT makes. Three NS defences held EW to -1. Only Pair 1 as EW found 9
tricks. Our ♦AJ1063 behind dummy's ♦Q95 was the critical holding — the same
pattern of sitting behind dummy's honours that characterises our defence.

## Board 13 — Penalty Double: 3♣X-1 | 8.0 MP

**Round 7, we are EW.** Defending North's preempt after penalty conversion.

```
              ♠ A4
              ♥ 72
              ♦ 106
              ♣ AJ106542  ← Declarer (North)
♠ 752                 ♠ 1086       ← Our East
♥ AQ1098              ♥ K6

♣ 8                   ♣ KQ73
              ♠ KQJ93      ← Our West
              ♥ J543
              ♦ K83
              ♣ 9
```

**Contract:** N 3♣X. **Result:** 3♣X-1 = +200 (TOP — only EW pair to double).

### Defence Decision: Penalty Conversion (DEC-9)

West reopened with a takeout double after North's 3♣ preempt (♠KQJ93, 11 HCP,
short clubs). East held ♣KQ73 behind North's 7-card club suit.

**SFS Analysis for penalty pass decision:**

East's ♣KQ73 defending 3♣:
- SFS = 1 (♣3 is the only free card; ♣K, ♣Q, ♣7 are all trick cards against
  declarer's ♣AJ106542)
- Trump trick count: ♣KQ73 behind ♣AJ106542 promotes at least 2 natural trump
  tricks. Declarer must lose to ♣K and ♣Q when finessing.
- East's total defensive tricks: ♣K + ♣Q + ♦A = 3 from East alone
- West's contribution: ♠KQJ93 + ♥AQ = at least 2-3 more tricks
- **Total: 5-6 defensive tricks against 3♣ (need 5 to set)** → PASS for penalty

### Dummy Analysis (DEC-8)

After the penalty pass, South becomes dummy. East sees:
- ♠KQJ93: **Running threat for the defence** — West's spade honours are offensive
  tricks for the defending side, not dummy threats
- ♥J543: **Neutral** — no running threat
- ♦K83: **Attack target** — East holds ♦A752 over it; ♦K is trapped
- ♣9: **Dead** — singleton club in dummy; no trump support for declarer

### Five Lines of Defence (DEC-8)

1. **Race to cash** — ♦A is a fast trick; ♠ tricks can be established
2. **Trump defence** — ♣KQ73 behind ♣AJ106542: natural trump promotion
3. **Passive** — after establishing position, let declarer play into trump tricks

### Opening Lead — West's Decision

West holds ♠KQJ93, ♥AQ1098, ♦QJ94, ♣8. Against 3♣X:

- ♠K: Top of sequence. Establishes spade tricks immediately. Partner's double
  doesn't promise spades, but ♠KQJ is a standing source of tricks.
- ♥A: Cash and see. Risk: gives declarer tempo.
- ♣8: Trump lead. Unusual against a penalty double — removes dummy's singleton
  but helps declarer draw OUR trumps.

**Best lead: ♠K.** Cash spade tricks, then switch to establish other winners.
Against a penalty double, you cash your winners and let the trump stack do the rest.

### Signal Freedom Score — Defensive Holdings

**East's ♣KQ73 (trumps):**
- SFS = 1 (♣3 free)
- When declarer leads trumps, East's plays are constrained — ♣K and ♣Q must
  win tricks on their own schedule
- **Silence Inference applies:** West sees East play ♣3 (forced) and infers
  trump honours from the inability to signal

**East's ♦A752:**
- SFS = 2 (♦7 and ♦2 free; ♦A and ♦5 border)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- When cashing ♦A: ♦7 (high = even count, 4 diamonds) signals length
- DEC-9 priority: count (dummy's ♦K83 threatens) > suit preference > attitude

**West's ♥AQ1098:**
- SFS = 2 (♥8 free, plus one of ♥10/♥9; ♥A and ♥Q are tricks)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- When discarding hearts: ♥8 (Italian SP: high even = higher suit preference)

### Critical Defensive Sequence

1. **Trick 1:** West leads ♠K. North ducks (♠A4). East plays ♠6 (low = standard
   discouraging? Or count: low = odd). No — East encourages with ♠6 if possible (reversed: low = encourage).
   From ♠1086: ♠6 = encouraging (reversed: low = encourage); ♠10 = discouraging (high = discourage).
2. **Trick 2:** West continues ♠Q. North wins ♠A.
3. **Tricks 3-6:** Declarer attacks trumps. Runs into ♣KQ73: East's ♣K and ♣Q
   each win a trick. That's already 3 tricks for the defence (♠K + ♣K + ♣Q).
4. **Tricks 7+:** ♦A (East) and ♥A (West) provide the 4th and 5th defensive tricks.

**Result: 3♣X-1 = +200.** Defence collects 5 tricks: ♠K, ♣K, ♣Q, ♦A, ♥A (minimum).

### Impact

Other EW pairs played spades (2♠= +110) or let 3♣ go undoubled (-100).
The double converted +100 into +200 — a 100-point gain for a full top.

**Key carding insight:** The penalty pass works because East can count defensive
tricks using the SFS framework. ♣KQ73 behind a preemptive 7-card suit = at least
2 natural trump tricks. ♦A = 3 from East alone. West's ♠KQJ93 + ♥AQ provides
2+ more. Total: 5+ defensive tricks = setting trick guaranteed. The decision is
mechanical, not heroic.

## T2 Summary — Carding Lessons

| Board | Type | DEC-7/8/9 Element | MP |
|------:|------|-------------------|---:|
| 6 | Heart defence (beat DD) | Dummy taxonomy (♦ running threat) + SFS + Obvious Shift | 8.0 |
| 11 | Heart defence (beat DD) | TSP + SFS + Silence Inference + Five Lines | 8.0 |
| 5 | NT defence (beat DD) | Reverse Smith Echo + 3rd/5th leads + Obvious Shift | 6.0 |
| 13 | Penalty conversion | SFS counting for penalty decision + trump stack | 8.0 |

### Dominant Pattern: Trump Holdings Behind Declarer

Boards 6 and 11 both feature trump holdings behind declarer:
- **Board 6:** South's ♥Q532 behind East's ♥K10976; dummy (West) ♥AJ doubleton.
  South's ♥Q is the surprise trick — declarer expects it onside.
- **Board 11:** East's ♥K74 behind South's ♥AQ963; dummy (North) ♥J85.
  East's ♥K is the natural trick — declarer can finesse but ♥K scores regardless.

In both cases, the off-trump holdings matter as much as the trump position:
- Board 6: ♦AJ behind dummy's ♦K10975 prevents the running threat
- Board 11: ♦AK provides fast tricks before declarer can strip

### Dummy Taxonomy Drives Defence Line

Every board's defence line follows directly from the dummy taxonomy:
- **Running threat** (♦K10975 on B6) → don't help establish it; lead passively
- **Attack target** (♦Q102 on B11, ♦Q95 on B5) → attack through it
- **Entry suit** (♣A108 on B11) → track entries, don't give tempo
- **Dead** (♣9 on B13) → dummy can't help declarer in trumps

### Reverse Smith Echo Debut

Board 5 is the first tournament application of cross-suit attitude about the
opening lead. South's ♦AJ1063 behind dummy's ♦Q95 made the Reverse Smith signal
unambiguous: LOW = continue diamonds. The NT defence benefited from sustained
diamond pressure.

### SFS Paradox in Action

Board 13 illustrates F-DEF-017 (SFS Paradox): East's ♣KQ73 has SFS=1 precisely
because it contains 3 trick cards. The more defensive power, the less signalling
freedom. The Silence Inference (F-DEF-018) converts this constraint into
information: West reads East's forced ♣3 as confirming trump honours.
