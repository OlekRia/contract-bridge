## Board 12 — DEVASTATING Penalty: 3♥X-4 = +800 | 4.0 MP

Round 3, we are NS. North = Ori, South = partner.

```
              ♠ AJ1097       ← Our North
              ♥ 2
              ♦ J974
              ♣ 653
♠ K843                ♠ 652
♥ 53                  ♥ QJ10976  ← Declarer (East)
♦ K10653              ♦ —
♣ 72                  ♣ AJ84
              ♠ Q            ← Our South
              ♥ AK84
              ♦ AQ82
              ♣ KQ109
```

**Contract:** E 3♥X nonvul. **Result:** -4 = +800 (TOP).

### Penalty Decision — SFS Counting (DEC-9)

South holds ♥AK84 behind East's hearts. Counting defensive tricks before doubling:

| Holding | Trick cards | Free cards | SFS | Defensive tricks |
|---------|------------|------------|----:|-----------------|
| ♥AK84   | ♥A, ♥K     | ♥8, ♥4     |   2 | 2 sure + promotion potential |
| ♦AQ82   | ♦A (♦Q positional) | ♦8, ♦2 | 2 | 1 sure + 1 if ♦K onside |
| ♣KQ109  | ♣K, ♣Q, ♣10 | ♣9       |   1 | 1-2 behind ♣AJ |
| ♠Q       | ♠Q (positional) | —     |   0 | 0-1 |

**Minimum from South alone:** ♥A + ♥K + ♦A + ♣K = 4 tricks.
**Likely total:** 5-6 (♦Q scores, ♣Q scores behind ♣A).
**North adds:** ♠AJ1097 = 1-2 spade tricks.
**Combined defence:** 6-7 tricks. East needs 9 for 3♥. Double is automatic.

### Dummy Analysis (DEC-8)

West tables: ♠K843, ♥53, ♦K10653, ♣72.

| Suit | Dummy | Taxonomy | Implication |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| ♠ K843 | 4 cards | Attack target | ♠K trapped under North's ♠AJ1097 |
| ♥ 53 | 2 cards | Trump holding | Minimal support; declarer has 7 trumps total at best |
| ♦ K10653 | 5 cards | Establishable source | BUT East has a VOID — completely useless |
| ♣ 72 | 2 cards | No threat | Doubleton, no honours |

**Five Lines:** RACE TO CASH. Only 2 dummy trumps. South's ♥AK84 = 4 trumps behind
declarer. Total trump control. Draw trumps, then cash side winners.

### Defence Execution

1. Cash ♥A, ♥K — draw dummy's trumps, still hold ♥84
2. Cash ♦A — fast trick before any establishment
3. Lead ♣K — attack declarer's only entry suit (♣AJ84)
4. Continue cashing — declarer has no play with trumps drawn and entries cut

**Result:** 9 needed, 5 made = -4. Nonvul doubled: 100+100+100+100+300+300 = +800.

### Signal Freedom — North's Discards

North must discard on South's trump leads. Holdings:

- ♠AJ1097: SFS = 3 (♠9, ♠7 free; ♠A, ♠J, ♠10 are tricks). Discard ♠7 (Italian:
  odd = interest in spades). Tells South: "I have spade tricks when trumps are done."
- ♦J974: SFS = 3 (♦9, ♦7, ♦4 free; ♦J marginal). Flexible signal cards.

**Silence Inference (DEC-7 §7):** South leads ♣K — sequence lead. North reads
♣KQ and knows South controls clubs. No signal needed; South claims the suit.

### Impact

Best single-board result across all 4 tournaments. Other tables: 3NT+1 (+630), 3NT-1 (-100).
Only we doubled 3♥ and collected the maximum. SFS counting enables penalty doubles.

## Board 22 — Set Vulnerable Game: 4♦-2 = +200 | 4.0 MP

```

              ♦ 5
              ♣ K1062
♠ AKJ7                ♠ 853

♦ AK87                ♦ QJ102
♣ QJ4                 ♣ A985
              ♠ 96           ← Our South
              ♥ AK963
              ♦ 9643
              ♣ 73
```

**Contract:** E 4♦ vul. **Result:** -2 = +200 (TOP).

### Opening Lead Analysis (DEC-7 §1)

South holds ♥AK963. From AK: lead ♥A (top of touching honours). Standard AK
agreement: ♥A asks for attitude.

### Dummy Analysis (DEC-8)

After ♥A lead, dummy (West) tables: ♠AKJ7, ♥104, ♦AK87, ♣QJ4.

| Suit | Dummy | Taxonomy | Implication |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| ♠ AKJ7 | 4 cards | Running threat | 3-4 fast spade tricks; race to cash before these run |
| ♥ 104 | 2 cards | Attack target | Our lead attacks; only 2 hearts, quick to exhaust |
| ♦ AK87 | 4 cards | Trump holding | Strong trumps, solid support |
| ♣ QJ4 | 3 cards | Establishable source | QJ sequence; ♣K location matters |

**Five Lines:** RACE TO CASH. Dummy's ♠AKJ7 is a running threat. Cash hearts NOW
before declarer draws trumps and runs spades.

### Signal Freedom — North at Trick 1

North holds ♥8752 under ♥A lead:

- SFS = 4 (all four hearts are free — no trick potential)
- Signal capacity: 2+ bits
- ♥2 (low = encouraging, reversed attitude): "Continue hearts. I have length."

Under Obvious Shift (DEC-7 §5, Granovetter), attitude at trick 1 refers to the
obvious shift suit. Obvious shift = clubs (QJ4 in dummy, neither running nor
solid). But urgent reading: hearts must be cashed NOW against ♠AKJ7 running threat.

### Defence Execution

1. ♥A — attitude from North (♥2, encouraging — reversed: low = encourage)
2. ♥K — cash second heart trick. North shows count.
3. ♣7 shift — find North's ♣K (the obvious shift suit from dummy analysis)
4. ♣K — 3rd defensive trick
5. Heart continuation or club — force a 4th and 5th trick

South's ♥AK + North's ♣K = 3 tricks. Finding tricks 4-5 against 27 HCP requires
the heart length (♥963 still live) and North's ♠Q behind dummy's ♠AKJ7.

**Result:** 10 needed, 8 made = -2 vulnerable = +200.

### Impact

EW has 27 HCP — should make game. Other tables: 3NT-1 (+100), 3NT+1 (-630).
We set the vulnerable game by 2 for the best defensive result in the field.

## Board 2 — Good NT Defence: 3NT-2 = +200 | 3.0 MP

Round 1, we are EW. West = partner, East = Ori.

```
              ♠ Q875
              ♥ AQ85
              ♦ J82
              ♣ AK
♠ K93                 ♠ 1062       ← Our East (Ori)
♥ K32                 ♥ J76
♦ K975                ♦ A1043
♣ Q94                 ♣ 765
              ♠ AJ4
              ♥ 1094
              ♦ Q6
              ♣ J10832
```

**Contract:** N 3NT. **Result:** -2 = +200 for EW (Good, 3.0 MP).

### Defence Analysis — We Are EW

NS has 27 HCP (N: 16, S: 11). North opened 1NT, South raised to 3NT.

**Opening Lead from West (partner):** With ♦K975, a diamond lead is natural —
4th best from longest suit. ♦5 lead (4th best, DEC-7 §1).

### Dummy Analysis (DEC-8) — East's View

After ♦5 lead, dummy (South) tables: ♠AJ4, ♥1094, ♦Q6, ♣J10832.

| Suit | Dummy | Taxonomy | Implication |
|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| ♠ AJ4 | 3 cards | Entry/threat | ♠A is entry; ♠J is finesse threat |
| ♥ 1094 | 3 cards | No threat | No honours to develop |
| ♦ Q6 | 2 cards | Attack target | Our lead attacks; ♦Q is finessable |
| ♣ J10832 | 5 cards | Establishable source | Long clubs, needs entries |

**Five Lines:** ATTACK ENTRIES. Declarer needs clubs to run (♣AK in North +
♣J10832 in South = 7 club tricks if connected). Cut communication between
North's ♣AK and South's ♣J10832.

### Key Defensive Holding — East's ♦A1043

**Rule of 11:** Partner led ♦5 (4th best). 11 - 5 = 6 cards above ♦5 outside
West's hand. Dummy has ♦Q6 (1 above ♦5). East has ♦A104 (3 above ♦5).
Declarer has 6 - 1 - 3 = 2 cards above ♦5.

East plays ♦A at trick 1 if dummy plays low, or ♦10 if dummy plays ♦Q (covering
cheaply, saving ♦A for later).

### Reverse Smith Echo Opportunity (DEC-7 §6)

After winning a diamond trick, if declarer leads clubs to establish the suit,
East plays LOW in clubs at first opportunity = Reverse Smith positive = "Continue
diamonds, I like partner's lead suit."

West sees East's low club and knows: return diamonds when you regain the lead.

### Signal Freedom — East's Holdings

- ♥J76: SFS = 2 (♥7, ♥6 free; ♥J potential trick). Can signal in hearts.
- ♣765: SFS = 3 (all free). Maximum signalling flexibility in clubs.
- ♠1062: SFS = 3 (all free). Can signal in spades if needed.

### Why 3NT Failed

NS's 27 HCP should make 3NT, but EW's diamond control (♦K975 + ♦A1043 = 8
diamonds between us) provides 4 diamond tricks once established. With ♥K32 in
West as an entry, the defence cashes out before declarer runs clubs.

**Defensive tricks:** ♦A + ♦K + 2 long diamonds + ♥K = 5 tricks. 3NT-2 = +200.

### Impact

Two NS pairs stretched to 3NT and went -2. Only pair 7's NS stopped at 2NT=.
Our diamond defence was textbook: 4th-best lead, Rule of 11, Reverse Smith Echo
(low = like partner's lead) to maintain partnership communication.

defending 4♥= (-420).

### Impact

aggressively in competitive auctions.

Setting 3NT is solid defence, but at pair 3's table NS set 4♥ by 2 (+100).
Our +50 is the middle result. The teaching point: Reverse Smith Echo at trick 2
(North plays low = like spades) ensures South's spade suit runs without miscommunication.

### Key Insights

**SFS counting enables penalty doubles.** Board 12: South counts 4+ trick cards
(♥AK, ♦A, ♣K) from own hand alone. The double is automatic regardless of
partner's holding. Best single-board result across all tournaments.

**Dummy taxonomy drives the Five Lines.** Board 22: ♠AKJ7 in dummy = running

establishable source → attack entries to cut communication.

**Reverse Smith Echo is a free signal.** Boards 2 and 24 both feature NT defence where
cross-suit attitude about the opening lead costs zero trick potential (SFS = max
in the signalling suit). Reverse Smith: LOW = like partner's lead suit, HIGH = dislike.
Reverse Smith Echo's value is now concrete across multiple boards.

**Competitive bidding through the defensive lens.** Board 25: knowing we can set
4♥ at most 1 (South's heart void, North's defensive ♠AQ) means 3♠ is a profitable
sacrifice even if it goes down. It made — earning the outright TOP.
