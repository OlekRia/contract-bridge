### Opening Lead Analysis (R-DEF-001)

**Non-standard lead.** Our East led ♦9 from ♦AQ86. West has a singleton ♦5

convention from AQxx. It is neither 4th-best (♦6), nor top of honour (♦A),

**Correct lead: ♦6 (4th best from AQ86).** This gives partner Rule of 11
information: 11-6 = 5 cards above the 6 outside East. Partner (West) sees ♦5
and can calculate declarer's holding. Alternatively, ♦A (aggressive, looking
at dummy) is defensible if East suspects ♦K is short in dummy.

The ♦9 gives no useful information and may suggest top-of-nothing or MUD,
confusing West completely.

**DEC-7 standard:** From AQxx, 4th-best is standard. From interior sequence
(like 1098x), lead top of sequence. The 9 from AQ86 fits neither pattern.

- ♦KJ94: **Establishable source** — our lead runs into KJ; declarer can finesse
- ♣KJ4: **Finesse threat** — KJ needs to guess position of A and Q

The ♦9 lead into dummy's ♦KJ94 is catastrophic: declarer plays low, the 9
forces West's nothing (♦5), and dummy's ♦KJ now dominate the suit.

### Signal Freedom Score (F-DEF-016)

- SFS = 0 (no free cards — the singleton is forced)
- Signal capacity: 0 bits — West cannot communicate anything in diamonds
- **Silence Inference (F-DEF-018):** West's forced play of ♦5 tells East

### Five Lines of Defence

**Line identification:** With dummy showing ♦KJ94 and ♣KJ4, declarer has two
finesse sources. EW should **race** — attack a side suit to establish tricks
before declarer can take all finesses.

**Preferred defence line:** Lead a major (♠J or ♥J from East). ♠J is top of
doubleton and attacks dummy's weakest suit. Even a heart lead (♥J) is better
than gifting the diamond finesse.

### Impact

2♥= (+110). Our non-standard ♦9 into dummy's ♦KJ94 helped declarer maximise

**Rule: From AQxx, lead 4th-best. Never lead a random middle card.**

**Contract:** E 3NT. **Our lead (South):** ♥10 (top of doubleton). **Result:** 3NT= = -600 (TOP).

### Opening Lead Analysis (R-DEF-001)

South has no natural lead — ♠762, ♥104, ♦9643, ♣9763. No sequence, no length
worth attacking. The ♥10 is a top-of-doubleton lead, but crucially it **hit
partner's ♥QJ96**.

**Why this works:** Against NT, when you have nothing, find partner's suit.
South cannot know North has ♥QJ96, but the ♥10 is the only card that has a
plausible sequence partner (♥10-9 would be ideal; here ♥10-4 is honest top
of doubleton). The alternatives — ♠7, ♦6, ♣9 — all walk into EW's combined
26 HCP.

**DEC-7 lead convention:** Top of doubleton = ~0.4 bits information (partner
knows you have exactly 2). But the PLACEMENT of the lead — into North's
♥QJ96 — is the real gain.

### Dummy Analysis After Lead (DEC-8, F-DEF-014)

After ♥10 lead, dummy (West): ♠KQ93, ♥K52, ♦AQ107, ♣J4.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- ♠KQ93: **Running threat** — can produce 3+ tricks if declarer gains tempo
- ♥K52: **Attack target** — our lead attacks this; ♥K is finessable by North's ♥QJ
- ♦AQ107: **Establishable source** — needs ♦K location (it's with North!)
- ♣J4: **No threat** — weak doubleton

North's ♥QJ96 behind dummy's ♥K52 = 3 heart tricks after the ♥10 opens the
suit. Declarer is caught: ♥K is trapped, and South's ♥10 forces declarer to
spend the ♥K or duck (letting North's ♥QJ win).

### Signal Freedom Score (F-DEF-016)

North holds ♥QJ96 after the ♥10 lead:
- SFS = 2 (♥9 and ♥6 are free; ♥Q and ♥J are trick cards)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit (binary)
- **Attitude only** (DEC-9 priority at SFS=2): ♥6 = encouraging (reversed: low = encourage), ♥9 = discouraging (high = discourage)
- No need for count or suit preference — attitude is all partner needs

### Five Lines of Defence

**Line identification: RACE.** North has ♥QJ96 established; the defence must
race to cash hearts before declarer runs 9 tricks in the black suits and
diamonds. After ♥10 lead → ♥Q wins (or ♥K played) → continue hearts to
establish 3-4 tricks.

### Smith Echo (R-DEF-013)

After hearts are established, when declarer plays a different suit, South
should give a **Smith echo** — low card in the new suit = "I liked my
opening lead, continue hearts when you regain the lead" (reverse Smith: low
= like, high = dislike). South's ♥4 return (if declarer ducks the first
heart) confirms the heart continuation.

### Impact

All 3 tables played 3NT. Other leads: ♣5 (-630), ♦6 (-630). Only our ♥10
held declarer to 9 tricks (-600). The lead saved 30 points and gained 3.0 MP
over the field.

**Rule confirmed: When you have nothing, lead the suit most likely to find
partner's holding. Top of doubleton is fine vs NT (R-DEF-001).**

**Information content:** The ♦10 lead tells partner:

**3rd/5th alternative:** Leading 3rd-highest (♦8) from ♦1098762 would be

Dummy (North): ♠63, ♥K753, ♦K, ♣Q87542.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- ♠63: **No threat** — declarer's trumps are in hand
- ♥K753: **Entry suit** — ♥K is a potential entry to clubs
- ♦K: **Attack target** — singleton king; our lead attacks this directly
- ♣Q87542: **Establishable source** — 6 cards but needs entries and ♣A is with declarer

- East wins ♦A over dummy's ♦K
- SFS = 1 (♦3 is the only free card; ♦A and ♦J are trick cards)
- East returns ♦J (top of remaining touching honour)
- **Silence Inference (F-DEF-018):** East's high-card play (♦A then ♦J) tells

**Line identification: RACE.** Declarer has 6 spade tricks in hand (♠KJ10985)
but needs entries to dummy for clubs. The defence must **race** to cash
diamond tricks before declarer can establish and reach dummy's clubs.

The ♦10 lead starts the race: ♦10 → dummy's ♦K → East's ♦A → ♦J return →
West's diamonds are now established. Declarer must spend tempo drawing trumps,
giving EW time to cash diamonds when they get in with ♠AQ.

**Critical entry management:** West's ♠AQ provides two trump entries to cash
diamond winners. The defence has: ♦A (trick 1), ♠A (entry), ♠Q (entry),
plus diamond winners = enough to set the contract.

### Obvious Shift (R-DEF-015)

After ♦A wins trick 1, East does NOT shift — East returns ♦J (suit
continuation, not an obvious shift). The obvious shift test: dummy's ♥K753

### Impact

**Rule confirmed: Lead your long suit. Top of interior sequence from length

♣AQJ52 — a free finesse that establishes declarer's best suit immediately.

**DEC-7 priority:** Sequence leads (KQ, QJ, J10, 109) are ALWAYS preferred

- ♦A98765: **Establishable source** — 6 cards with ace; this is declarer's

- ♣K6: **Entry** — ♣K is an entry to dummy's diamonds

The ♦K lead is perfect: it forces ♦A immediately or establishes ♦Q as a

- SFS = 2 (both ♦3 and ♦2 are free — no trick potential)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit

  ♦3 (low = odd count, 1 remaining after following once) → helps North

### Five Lines of Defence

♦A98765 for 5+ tricks. North must race to set up ♦KQ before declarer can
run diamonds.

After ♦K lead (♦A wins from dummy), North still has ♦Q4. When North regains
the lead via ♥AK, the ♦Q cashes. This holds declarer to 9 tricks.

**Contrast with actual ♣4 lead:** The ♣4 into ♣AQJ52 gives declarer 5 club
tricks immediately plus ♦A and ♠AQ = 9+ tricks with no defensive timing.

### Obvious Shift (R-DEF-015)

After ♦K lead and ♦A win, if declarer plays a heart to duck to North's ♥AK:
- North cashes ♦Q (suit continuation, not a shift)
- The obvious shift suit would be spades (♠10654 — length), but diamonds

### Impact

and held declarer to -600. Our ♣4 into ♣AQJ52 gifted declarer an overtrick.

**Rule confirmed: Always lead from sequences (KQ, QJ, etc.) over from nothing
(xxx). Same error pattern as Board 8 — leading weakness into strength.**

## Board 22 — PERFECT Lead: ♦A vs 6♠ Slam | 3.0 MP

**We are EW (Round 5).** East deals, EW vul.

```
              ♠ J1032
              ♥ AQJ7
              ♦ 62
              ♣ A87
♠ 7          ← Our West   ♠ Q86        ← Our East
♥ K10632                   ♥ 9854
♦ A1054                    ♦ 93
♣ J93                      ♣ 10654
              ♠ AK954
              ♥ —
              ♦ KQJ87
              ♣ KQ2
```

**Contract:** S 6♠. **Our lead (West):** ♦A. **Result:** 6♠-1 = -50 (GOOD).

### Opening Lead Analysis (R-DEF-001)

Against a slam, cashing an ace is often correct when you suspect declarer has
a loser there. West heard South bid strongly through diamonds (South has
♦KQJ87 — a 5-card suit). The auction likely revealed diamond length but
not solidity.

**DEC-7 slam lead protocol:** Against suit slams, lead an ace when:
1. You suspect declarer has losers in this suit — confirmed (♦A over ♦KQJ)
2. There's no better passive alternative — West has no sequence elsewhere
3. Partner may have a trick to cash — East's ♦93 gives nothing, but the ♦A
   alone sets the contract

West holds ♦A1054 and ♥K10632. The ♥K lead is tempting (hoping to cash it)
but dummy likely has heart honours. The ♦A is the safe cash.

### Dummy Analysis After Lead (DEC-8, F-DEF-014)

After ♦A lead, dummy (North): ♠J1032, ♥AQJ7, ♦62, ♣A87.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- ♠J1032: **Trump support** — 4 trumps with J; good support for declarer
- ♥AQJ7: **Running threat** — 4 cards with AQJ; can produce 3-4 tricks via
  finesse (but declarer is void!)
- ♦62: **Dead suit** — our ♦A cashed; dummy has no more useful diamonds
- ♣A87: **Entry** — ♣A is a certain entry to dummy

### Signal Freedom Score (F-DEF-016)

East holds ♦93 after the ♦A lead:
- SFS = 2 (both ♦9 and ♦3 are free — no trick potential)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- **Suit preference** (priority signal when partner cashes ace vs slam):
  ♦9 (high = hearts, the higher-ranking side suit) or ♦3 (low = clubs)
- East should play ♦3 — no preference for either suit, so default low

### Obvious Shift (R-DEF-015, Granovetter)

After ♦A cashes the setting trick, West must decide trick 2:

**Obvious shift identification from dummy:**
- ♥AQJ7 = running threat. **DO NOT** shift to hearts — this helps declarer
- ♣A87 = entry, not attackable
- ♠J1032 = trump — no shift into trump
- **No obvious shift exists** — dummy is too strong. West should exit passively.

West exits with a trump or a club, forcing declarer to manage the hand
without the missing diamond trick.

### Five Lines of Defence

**Line identification: PASSIVE after cashing.** The ♦A is the only defensive
trick. After cashing it, the defence has no more natural tricks (♥K is
behind dummy's ♥AQJ7 and will be finessed). The correct line is: cash ♦A,
then exit passively (trump or low club) and wait.

There is no second trick to develop — just don't give declarer anything extra.

### Impact

DD says 6♠ makes (12 tricks) with best play, but our ♦A lead removed
declarer's timing and may have disrupted the communication needed for
the heart-diamond squeeze or endplay. Both slam tables went down. The third
table only bid 4♠+2 (+480).

**Rule confirmed: Cash aces vs slams when the auction suggests a loser there.**

### Actionable Rules (Confirmed by T1 Evidence)

1. **R-DEF-001 (sequence priority):** From KQx, QJx, J10x, 109x — ALWAYS
   lead top of sequence. This is the highest-information lead (~1.0 bit).
   Evidence: Board 20 (violated, -3 MP), Board 11 (followed, +4 MP).

2. **R-DEF-001 (length priority):** Lead your longest suit vs NT when it
   contains a sequence. Evidence: Board 11 (♦10 from 1098762, +4 MP).

3. **R-DEF-001 (no singletons vs NT):** Never lead a singleton in a side
   suit vs notrump. Evidence: Board 8 (♠J singleton, -4 MP).

4. **R-DEF-001 (4th best from AQxx):** From AQxx without interior sequence,
   lead 4th-best for Rule of 11. Evidence: Board 4 (♦9 non-standard, -2 MP).

5. **DEC-7 slam protocol:** Cash aces vs suit slams when the auction suggests
   a loser. Evidence: Board 22 (♦A, +1 MP vs field).

6. **DEC-9 (find partner):** When you have nothing, lead the suit most likely
   to find partner. Top of doubleton is acceptable. Evidence: Board 10
   (♥10, +3 MP).
