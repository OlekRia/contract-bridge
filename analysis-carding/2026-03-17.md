this holding (top of sequence DQ, or 4th-best D4, or 3rd D8). South holds D965

Defending against E 3H, dummy is West: S9763, HA10, DAK1073, C103.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- S9763: **No threat** — small spades, no fast tricks
- HA10: **Trump holding** — only 2 trumps, HA is a sure winner for declarer
- DAK1073: **Running threat** — 5-card diamond suit with AK, but declarer (East) has a diamond VOID
- C103: **No threat** — small clubs

**Critical observation:** Declarer has a diamond void. Dummy's DAK1073 cannot
provide discards unless declarer draws trumps first and ruffs into dummy.
This means declarer must rely on cross-ruffing or establishing a side suit.

**Five Lines of Defence:**
1. Dummy has DAK1073 but declarer is void — declarer needs to ruff diamonds
   to reach dummy. **CUT RUFFS** by leading trumps? Our trumps (HQJ84 South,
   HK7 North) are strong enough to consider this.
2. **RACE TO CASH:** NS hold CA, CQ, SA — cash these before declarer can

- **Silence Inference (F-DEF-018):** North can only play H7 as a free card.
  South sees H7 and infers North has a heart honour (can't signal with it).

**South's C5:**

- **Silence Inference:** South's C5 is forced. North sees C5 and knows

**North's CAQ842:**
- SFS = 2 (C8 and C2 are free; CA, CQ, C4 are trick cards)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- After cashing CA, North continues CQ (sequence) or switches

**South's SQJ1052:**
- SFS = 2 (S5 and S2 are free; SQ, SJ, S10 are trick/sequence cards)
- On a spade switch: S5 (discouraging) or S2 (count) depending on context

### Defence Execution

NS have: SA (1), HK (1), plus potential club tricks from CAQ842. Setting

### Impact

**This is NT — Reverse Smith Echo applies at trick 2+.**

After the C5 lead, suppose declarer wins CA and plays diamonds. South holds
D52 and must follow suit.

South's D52: SFS = 1 (D2 is free; D5 is borderline). On the first diamond:
- D2 (lower) = Reverse Smith positive = "I like the club lead, return clubs"
- D5 (higher) = Reverse Smith negative

**Five Lines of Defence: RACE TO CASH.**

### Signal Freedom Score

## Board 14 — Sharp Lead: SJ vs 3S | 6.0 MP

```
              S J2           <- Our North
              H A942
              D A84
              C AQ105
S Q109764             S AK8
H 753                 H K1086
D QJ105               D 76
C --                   C J742
              S 53          <- Our South
              H QJ
              D K932
              C K9863
```

**Contract:** E 3S. **Our lead (North):** SJ. **Result:** 3S-1 = +50 (TOP).

### Opening Lead Analysis (R-DEF-001)

North holds SJ2 — a doubleton in the opponent's suit. Leading the SJ
(top of doubleton) into declarer's trump suit is counterintuitive but
has a specific purpose: **Trump Attack.**

**Why this works against this specific layout:**
1. Declarer (East) holds SAK8 — only 3 trumps
2. Dummy (West) holds SQ109764 — 6 trumps with QJ10 sequence
3. The SJ forces a decision: declarer must spend SAK to draw trumps
4. With only 3 trumps in hand (East) and North's SJ removing one round,
   declarer loses trump control

**DEC-7 classification:** Top of doubleton in trumps = trump lead for
passive defence. Information content: partner sees SJ from North and
infers doubleton (or singleton J, less likely).

### Dummy Analysis (DEC-8)

After SJ lead, dummy is West: SQ109764, H753, DQJ105, C-- (VOID).

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- SQ109764: **Trump holding** — 6 trumps with QJ109, strong support.
  Declarer's 3-card SAK8 is the short trump hand
- H753: **No threat** — small hearts
- DQJ105: **Establishable source** — QJ sequence needs entries; South's
  DK932 sits behind it
- C-- (VOID): **Ruff threat** — dummy can ruff every club! This is the
  PRIMARY THREAT

**Five Lines of Defence: CUT RUFFS + FORCE DECLARER.**

The club void in dummy is critical. If declarer plays clubs, dummy ruffs
for free tricks. But declarer (East) holds CJ742 — modest clubs. The
real danger: declarer leads clubs from hand and ruffs in the 6-trump
dummy, which does NOT cost trump tricks (dummy has 6, hand has 3).

However, with only 3 trumps in hand (SAK8), declarer is vulnerable to
a **forcing defence**: lead a suit declarer must ruff in hand, depleting
the short trump holding.

### Signal Freedom Score

**South's S53:**
- SFS = 1 (S3 is free; S5 is borderline)
- On North's SJ lead: South plays S3 (count — showing odd number)

**North's HA942:**
- SFS = 2 (H9 and H2 are free; HA and H4 are trick/useful cards)
- Signal capacity: 1 bit
- On a heart switch: H2 (encouraging — reversed: low = encourage, "I have the ace"); H9 = discouraging (high = discourage)

**South's DK932:**
- SFS = 2 (D9 and D2 are free; DK, D3 are trick/useful cards)
- DK sits behind dummy's DQJ105 — excellent position

### Obvious Shift (DEC-7 S5, R-DEF-015)

After SJ lead and seeing dummy with C void and DQJ105:

**Obvious shift suit = diamonds.** Dummy has DQJ105 — an establishable
source. But South's DK932 sits directly behind it. Leading diamonds
through dummy attacks this holding while South's DK captures.

Hearts are secondary — dummy has H753 (no threat), and attacking hearts
only helps if we can cash HA quickly.

### Defence Execution

1. SJ lead removes a trump round
2. Win a trick, switch to diamonds through dummy's DQJ105
3. South's DK captures; continue diamonds to force declarer
4. Cash HA, CAQ when in

Setting 3S by 1: SA (already played), HA, DA (or DK capturing), and
club tricks from CAQ105 + CK9863. Defence has abundant tricks.

### Impact

Every other NS pair went minus (3S= -140, 3C-2 -100, 4SX= -590). We were
the only defenders to set the spade contract. The trump lead neutralised
declarer's ruffing power and the club void in dummy was never exploited.

North holds SAKQ82 — a 5-card suit with the top three honours. Against NT:

**DEC-7 lead convention:** From AKQ82, lead SA (top of honour sequence).
This is textbook: AKQ is a solid sequence, lead the top.

**Information content:** ~1.5 bits. Partner sees SA and knows North holds AKQ
(or AK minimum). The follow-up with SK and SQ confirms 5-card suit.

**3rd/5th alternative:** Does NOT apply — this is a sequence lead, not a

East is declarer, so North leads blind, then dummy (West) appears:
S1053, HAQ9, DKQ43, CA107.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**

  No spade tricks for declarer
- HAQ9: **Running threat** — AQ tenace + H9. If declarer finds HK or HJ,

- DKQ43: **Establishable source** — KQ touching, needs the DA (South has it).
  4 diamonds potentially
- CA107: **Entry suit** — CA is an entry; C107 provides secondary tricks

- Signal capacity: 1 bit

- Then S7, S6 follow as North runs the suit

**No unblocking needed** — South has S976, all small. Just follow suit
in natural order.

**Reverse Smith Echo is irrelevant here** — North is running spades, no need for
cross-suit attitude. The lead suit IS the suit being established.

### Defence Execution

1. SA -> SK -> SQ -> S8 -> S2: Run 5 spade tricks
2. Declarer (East) has SJ4 — follows S4, SJ, then discards begin
3. Dummy (West) has S1053 — follows S3, S5, S10, then discards
4. After 5 spade tricks, switch to D9 (find South's DA10875)
5. South's DA = trick 6. Continue D10 = trick 7.

5 spade tricks + DA + possibly another = 7 tricks. 1NT-2 confirmed.

### Impact

The only NS plus score in the field. Other results:

- 2S-1 -100 (NS played spades and went down)

Dummy is East: SQ, HAJ943, DAJ104, CA53.

**Dummy Suit Taxonomy:**
- SQ: **Singleton** — one spade trick at most; declarer (West) has SAKJ82

prevent this suit from running if declarer has entries. The C7 lead was

## 4. Reverse Smith Echo Applicability (DEC-7 S6, R-DEF-013)

Reverse Smith Echo is a free signal available only vs NT contracts at trick 2+.
**Polarity: LOW = like the lead (positive), HIGH = dislike the lead (negative).**

| Board | Lead Suit | Reverse Smith Moment | Free Card Used | Value |
|-------|-----------|----------------------|----------------|-------|
| T4 Bd8 | Clubs (C5) | South follows to diamonds | D2 from D52 (Reverse Smith positive = "return clubs") | High |
| T2 Bd5 | Spades | South plays low at trick 2 | Low card = "return spades" | High |
| T3 Bd24 | Spades | South plays low on diamond | Low = "return spades" | Medium |

**When Reverse Smith Echo applies:**
- Contract is NT (not suit)
- Trick 2 or later
- Following suit in a NON-led suit
- Partner has SFS >= 1 in the follow suit

**When Reverse Smith Echo does NOT apply:**
- Suit contracts (use standard signals)
- Running your own suit (T4 Bd15 — running SAKQ, no Reverse Smith needed)
- SFS = 0 in follow suit (forced play, Silence Inference instead)

## 7. Elite Heart Defence Pattern (T2-T4)

Across T2-T4, we consistently set heart contracts that the field allowed to make:

| Board | Our Holdings | Declarer's Hearts | Result | MP |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|--------|---:|
| T2 Bd6 | HAJ behind declarer | HK10976 | 4H-1 | 8.0 |
| T2 Bd11 | HK74 behind declarer | HAQ963 | 4H-1 | 8.0 |
| T4 Bd1 | HK7 (N) + HQJ84 (S) vs HA10 (W) + H96532 (E) | E declarer in 3H | 3H-2 | 6.0 |

This is a reproducible skill rooted in:
1. **Accurate bidding reconstruction** — knowing declarer's trump length from the auction
2. **SFS-aware trump play** — timing the trump trick correctly; with SFS=1 (HK7), the
   H7 is a forced play that triggers Silence Inference
3. **Dummy taxonomy** — identifying which side suits to attack first (Bd1: clubs before
   declarer can establish diamonds through ruffs; Bd14: deny club ruff via trump lead)
4. **Silence Inference** — reading partner's forced trump plays as information about
   honour holdings, not as attitude signals
5. **Void awareness** — Bd1's declarer had a diamond void despite dummy holding DAK1073;
   Bd14's dummy had a club void. Both required adjusting the standard defence plan
